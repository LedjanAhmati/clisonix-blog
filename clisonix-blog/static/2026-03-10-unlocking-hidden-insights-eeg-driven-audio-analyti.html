The Problem: Challenges in Enterprise AI

Enterprise AI has made tremendous progress in recent years, but it still faces significant challenges. One of the major hurdles is integrating diverse data sources and modalities into a single, cohesive architecture. For instance, electroencephalography (EEG) signals from patients can provide valuable insights into their cognitive and emotional states, but processing and analyzing these data streams in real-time poses significant technical difficulties.

Another challenge is ensuring consistent state across distributed healthcare nodes, which requires robust synchronization mechanisms to maintain data integrity and reliability. Furthermore, the complexity of AI models and algorithms often leads to long training times, high computational costs, and suboptimal performance.
