The Problem: Real Challenges in Sustainable Tech

Healthcare is one of the most resource-intensive industries globally, with a significant carbon footprint attributed to medical equipment, data centers, and transportation. Moreover, the sheer volume of medical data generated daily poses significant challenges for storage, processing, and interpretation.

In recent years, researchers have explored EEG as a potential solution to overcome these obstacles. By analyzing brain activity patterns, EEG can provide valuable insights into patient conditions without the need for invasive procedures or large amounts of equipment. However, current EEG-based systems often rely on manual analysis, which is time-consuming and prone to human error.
