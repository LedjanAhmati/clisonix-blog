The Problem

Traditional methods for mental health assessment often rely on subjective evaluations and self-reported data, which can be prone to biases and inaccuracies. Audio analysis, on the other hand, offers a unique opportunity to tap into an individual's vocal patterns, tone, and speech characteristics, providing a more nuanced understanding of their emotional state.

However, real-time audio processing is not without its challenges. The sheer volume of data generated by audio streams can be overwhelming, requiring sophisticated algorithms to process and analyze the information in real-time. Moreover, maintaining consistent state across distributed healthcare nodes while ensuring seamless integration with existing infrastructure adds another layer of complexity.

Technical Deep Dive

To address these challenges, Clisonix has developed a comprehensive architecture that weaves together EEG, audio, and biosensor streams using our Signal Fabric technology. This allows for the creation of a unified dataset that can be analyzed in real-time to extract valuable insights into an individual's mental state.

At the heart of our system lies the Tide Engine, which ensures consistent state across distributed healthcare nodes. By utilizing a distributed ledger approach, we're able to maintain a shared understanding of the patient's data, ensuring seamless collaboration between healthcare professionals and institutions.

Our audio processing pipeline is built on top of the TensorFlow framework, utilizing the popular WaveNet architecture to model the acoustic features of speech. This enables us to extract rich, time-domain representations of an individual's vocal patterns, which can be used to detect subtle changes in their emotional state.
