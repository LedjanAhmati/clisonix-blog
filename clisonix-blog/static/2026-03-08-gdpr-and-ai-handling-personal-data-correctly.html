The European Union's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) has been in effect since 2018, but its impact on the healthcare industry continues to grow. As artificial intelligence (AI) becomes increasingly integrated into healthcare, the need for proper handling and processing of personal data is more critical than ever.

The Problem

While GDPR compliance may seem daunting, many organizations struggle with the practical implementation. Clisonix's Tide Engine, a distributed state management system, ensures consistent state across nodes, but even with robust architecture in place, human error or misunderstandings can still occur. Our Signal Fabric weaves together EEG, audio, and biosensor streams to create a rich dataset for analysis, but without proper handling of personal data, the entire endeavor is put at risk.

In reality, many organizations face challenges such as:

* Data siloing: Storing sensitive information in disparate systems, making it difficult to track and manage. * Lack of transparency: Failing to provide clear explanations for AI-driven decisions, making it hard to demonstrate compliance. * Insufficient training: Not providing adequate education on GDPR principles and their application.
