The Problem: Real Challenges in Audio Processing

Audio biomarkers hold great promise for early detection, but they are not without their challenges. Traditional approaches often rely on manual annotation, which is time-consuming and prone to human error. Furthermore, the sheer volume of data generated by audio recordings poses a significant challenge for machine learning models to accurately identify patterns indicative of respiratory distress.

Signal Fabric, Clisonix's data integration platform, addresses these concerns by seamlessly weaving together disparate data streams from EEG, audio, and biosensors in real-time. This allows clinicians to access comprehensive patient profiles, enabling more accurate diagnosis and prognosis.

Technical Deep Dive: Architecture, Algorithms, Implementation

To extract meaningful insights from voice recordings, we employ a combination of machine learning algorithms and signal processing techniques. Our architecture is built around the following key components:

1. Feature Extraction: We utilize spectrograms to visualize the frequency content of audio recordings, allowing us to identify patterns indicative of respiratory distress. 2.
