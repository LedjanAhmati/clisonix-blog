As the healthcare industry continues to adopt cutting-edge technologies like electroencephalography (EEG) and artificial intelligence (AI), concerns about patient data privacy are mounting. The consequences of a breach can be severe: loss of trust, reputational damage, and even physical harm to patients. In this article, we'll delve into the complexities of EEG data security, exploring the challenges, technical solutions, and real-world results.

The Problem

EEG technology has revolutionized the diagnosis and treatment of neurological disorders by providing a non-invasive window into brain activity. However, the high sensitivity and specificity of EEG signals pose unique security risks. If not properly protected, these sensitive data streams can be intercepted, tampered with, or even used for malicious purposes.

To illustrate this challenge, consider a scenario where an unauthorized third party gains access to a patient's EEG data, containing sensitive information about their brain activity. This could lead to identity theft, misuse of treatment plans, or even exploitation for targeted advertising.
