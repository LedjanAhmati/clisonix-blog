In recent years, the field of audiology has witnessed a paradigm shift towards incorporating cutting-edge technologies that harness the power of neuroscience to improve hearing healthcare. At Clisonix, we're pushing this boundary even further by developing EEG-inspired soundscapes that not only enhance auditory experiences but also contribute significantly to sustainable wellness. This article delves into our innovative approach and its implications for the future of neuro-powered audiology.

The Problem

Traditional hearing aids often focus on amplifying external sounds without addressing the complexities of brain processing or considering the environmental impact of these devices. Moreover, current audiological practices have been criticized for their lack of personalization, relying heavily on standardized tests that fail to capture individual differences in auditory perception and cognitive ability. These limitations hinder the effectiveness of current treatments and neglect the growing concern for sustainability in healthcare.

Technical Deep Dive

Our solution is built upon two key technologies: Signal Fabric, which seamlessly integrates EEG signals with audio streams and biosensor data, providing a holistic view of an individual's auditory experience; and Tide Engine, our proprietary system ensuring consistent state across distributed healthcare nodes. This synergy enables the creation of personalized soundscapes that adapt to an individual's brain activity in real-time.

The architecture is built around a deep learning framework, where neural networks process EEG data alongside audio inputs to generate adaptive soundscapes. These soundscapes are designed to mimic natural environments, promoting a sense of relaxation and rejuvenation while also addressing specific auditory impairments. The integration of Signal Fabric allows us to incorporate real-time feedback from biosensors, ensuring that the generated soundscapes are tailored to each individual's unique physiological response.

Machine learning network — Photo: Unsplash

The algorithmic backbone is founded on LIAM (Labor Intelligence Algorithm Model), which we have successfully implemented in various healthcare applications. For audiology, LIAM enables the computation of complex patterns from multi-modal data, offering insights into auditory perception that were previously inaccessible.
