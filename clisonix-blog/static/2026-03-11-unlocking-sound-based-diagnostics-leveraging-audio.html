The Problem: Real Challenges in Audio Processing

Audio data is inherently noisy, varying significantly across different environments and devices. Traditional signal processing techniques often fail to account for these variations, leading to suboptimal results. Furthermore, audio signals contain both frequency and time-domain features, requiring specialized algorithms that can extract meaningful insights from this complex data.

To address these challenges, researchers have turned to deep learning techniques, such as convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs). However, training these models requires large datasets of annotated audio signals, which are often scarce in the healthcare domain. Moreover, real-time processing and deployment on edge devices pose significant constraints.
