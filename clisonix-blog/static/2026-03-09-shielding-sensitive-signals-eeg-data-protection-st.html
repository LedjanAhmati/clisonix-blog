The Problem: Challenges in Data Privacy

EEG data protection poses unique challenges due to the nature of these signals. Unlike other medical records, EEG data is highly sensitive, containing intimate details about a patient's brain activity. Moreover, EEG signals are typically collected over extended periods, making them vulnerable to potential breaches. Healthcare institutions face significant hurdles in ensuring data privacy and security:

1. Data Integrity: Protecting EEG signals from unauthorized access or tampering. 2. Confidentiality: Safeguarding sensitive information contained within EEG data. 3. Compliance: Adhering to regulatory requirements, such as HIPAA.
