The Problem

Current efforts to create eco-efficient healthcare infrastructure often result in trade-offs between sustainability and performance. Traditional approaches prioritize energy-efficient systems and materials over human-centered design principles. This narrow focus neglects the profound impact that employee well-being, stress levels, and overall work environment have on patient care quality and staff retention.

For instance:

1. Space Utilization: Many healthcare facilities struggle to balance patient flow with the need for open spaces, leading to inefficient use of resources. 2. Lighting and Ventilation: While energy-efficient lighting systems can save costs, they often compromise natural light exposure, further exacerbating employee fatigue and stress. 3. Noise Pollution: Insufficient soundproofing measures can lead to disturbing levels of noise pollution, affecting both staff productivity and patient recovery.

These issues underscore the pressing need for a holistic approach that integrates AI-powered EEG with healthcare infrastructure design.
