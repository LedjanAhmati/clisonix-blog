The Problem

EEG (electroencephalography) data is highly sensitive and contains intimate details about an individual's brain activity. With the proliferation of wearable devices, mobile apps, and AI-driven diagnostic tools, EEG data is being generated at an unprecedented rate. However, this surge in data also creates new challenges for data protection.

The primary concerns revolve around:

1. Data breaches: Unauthorized access to sensitive EEG data can compromise patient confidentiality and lead to severe consequences. 2. Informed consent: Patients may not fully understand the implications of sharing their EEG data, leaving them vulnerable to exploitation. 3. Transparency and accountability: The lack of clear guidelines and regulations creates an environment where healthcare providers must navigate a complex web of laws and standards.

FAQ

❓ What are the key differences between symmetric and asymmetric encryption?

Symmetric encryption (e.g., AES) uses the same key for both encryption and decryption, while asymmetric encryption (e.g., RSA or elliptic curve) employs a public-private key pair for secure key exchange.

❓ How does Clisonix's Signal Fabric solution handle EEG data from multiple sources?

Our Signal Fabric weaves together EEG, audio, and biosensor streams in real-time using a proprietary algorithm, ensuring seamless integration of diverse data feeds.
