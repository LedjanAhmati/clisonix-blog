The Problem: Energy Consumption in Healthcare

Healthcare institutions are among the largest consumers of energy globally, with significant environmental footprints resulting from medical equipment, transportation, and facility operations. Moreover, healthcare technology often relies on high-performance computing, large storage needs, and frequent software updates – all of which exacerbate energy consumption.

For instance, studies have shown that a single MRI machine can consume up to 75 kW of power, equivalent to the energy usage of approximately 500 households [1]. Furthermore, data centers housing healthcare applications account for an estimated 2% of global carbon emissions [2].
