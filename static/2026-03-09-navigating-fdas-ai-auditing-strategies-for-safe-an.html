As the healthcare industry continues to integrate artificial intelligence (AI) into clinical workflows, the US Food and Drug Administration (FDA) is increasing its scrutiny on AI-driven devices. With the introduction of the FDA's new guidelines for AI auditing, developers must ensure that their EEG algorithms meet stringent regulatory requirements to avoid costly delays and penalties.

The Problem

Developing and deploying safe and effective EEG-based diagnostic tools is a complex task, made more challenging by the need for compliance with FDA regulations. The lack of transparency in AI decision-making processes, coupled with concerns about data security and bias, creates significant hurdles for developers. Current EEG algorithms often rely on manual feature extraction and machine learning techniques that are difficult to interpret and validate.
