The Problem: Challenges in Signal Processing

EEG signals are notoriously difficult to process due to their high noise levels, variable frequency ranges, and non-stationary nature. These characteristics make it challenging to design algorithms that can effectively extract meaningful information from these signals. Moreover, EEG data is often fragmented, making it difficult to identify patterns and trends.

To address these challenges, we need advanced signal processing techniques that can handle the complexities of EEG data.
