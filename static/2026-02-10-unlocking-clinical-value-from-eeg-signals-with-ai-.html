In the rapidly evolving landscape of healthcare technology, AI-powered signal processing has emerged as a vital component in unlocking clinical value from complex biomedical data. The electroencephalogram (EEG) signal, in particular, holds immense promise for diagnosing neurological disorders and monitoring patient health. However, extracting meaningful insights from these signals poses significant challenges.

The Problem: Real Challenges in Signal Processing

Existing EEG signal processing techniques often rely on manual feature extraction, which is time-consuming, subjective, and prone to human error. Moreover, traditional methods may not account for the intricate relationships between different frequency bands and their corresponding cognitive functions. These limitations hinder the development of accurate diagnostic models and prevent clinicians from making informed decisions.
