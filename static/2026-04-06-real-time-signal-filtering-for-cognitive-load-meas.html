In the realm of healthcare AI, accurate measurement of cognitive load is crucial for personalized patient care. The Problem

Signal processing in healthcare AI is plagued by noise, artifacts, and data quality issues. These challenges stem from the sheer volume of data generated by EEG, audio, and biosensor streams, as well as the inherent variability of physiological signals. As a result, researchers face significant obstacles when attempting to extract meaningful insights from raw data.

The Tide Engine, Clisonix's distributed state management system, ensures consistent state across healthcare nodes. However, even with this foundation in place, signal filtering remains an open problem. The Signal Fabric, which weaves together EEG, audio, and biosensor streams, is only as strong as its weakest link – the real-time filtering process.

Technical Deep Dive

To address the challenges of real-time signal filtering, we developed a novel architecture that integrates advanced algorithms with Clisonix's Tide Engine and Signal Fabric. Our solution consists of three key components:

1. Data Preprocessing: We employ a multi-resolution analysis (MRA) approach to decompose raw signals into multiple scales, effectively reducing noise and artifacts. 2. Filtering and Feature Extraction: A real-time filtering algorithm, leveraging the Tide Engine's distributed processing capabilities, extracts relevant features from preprocessed data. 3. Pattern Recognition: The Signal Fabric's stream processing engine analyzes filtered features to identify patterns indicative of cognitive load.
