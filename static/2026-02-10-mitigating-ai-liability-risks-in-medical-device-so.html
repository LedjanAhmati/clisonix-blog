The Problem

In recent years, AI has become an integral part of healthcare technology, enabling medical devices to provide more accurate diagnoses, personalized treatments, and efficient patient care. However, this increased reliance on AI also brings new risks and challenges. The lack of standardization in AI development, combined with the complexities of regulatory frameworks, creates a perfect storm for liability.

One notable challenge is the uncertainty surrounding data ownership and usage rights. As medical devices collect vast amounts of sensitive patient data, there's a pressing need to establish clear guidelines on how this data should be handled, stored, and used. Furthermore, the FDA has emphasized the importance of ensuring that AI algorithms are transparent, explainable, and free from biases.
