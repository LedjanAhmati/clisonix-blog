Audio processing is an inherently complex task. Voice assistants rely on algorithms to recognize and interpret spoken language, but these algorithms can be prone to errors. Coherence analysis, however, offers a new perspective on this problem. By examining the relationships between different frequency components in audio signals, coherence analysis can identify patterns that traditional algorithms often miss.

Consider this example: Imagine you're using a voice assistant to make a phone call, but the audio quality is poor due to background noise or interference. Traditional algorithms might struggle to separate the signal from the noise, leading to dropped calls and frustration for users. Coherence analysis, on the other hand, can help identify the underlying patterns in the audio signal, allowing for more effective noise reduction and improved overall quality.
