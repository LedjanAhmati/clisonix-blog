Among these signals, electroencephalography (EEG) stands out as a promising modality for real-time monitoring and early detection of neurological disorders. However, accurate signal processing is crucial for extracting meaningful insights from EEG data.

The Problem: Challenges in Signal Processing

Despite the potential of EEG-based mental health assessment, several challenges hinder its widespread adoption. One critical issue is the presence of noise and artifacts in raw EEG signals, which can compromise the accuracy of extracted features. Moreover, EEG signals are inherently high-dimensional, making it challenging to develop efficient filtering methods that balance signal preservation with computational complexity.
