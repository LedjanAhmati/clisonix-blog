Sound wave visualization — Photo: Unsplash The Problem

Real-world challenges in audio processing include:

* Noise reduction: Background noises like humming refrigerators or air conditioning units can significantly degrade speech recognition accuracy. * Echo cancellation: Reverb and echo effects can make it difficult for voice assistants to accurately recognize spoken commands. * Dynamic range compression: The human ear can detect a wide range of sound intensities, but most audio processing systems struggle to compress these dynamics without introducing artifacts.

To address these challenges, we need a more sophisticated approach to audio processing. This is where coherence analysis comes in – a powerful technique that analyzes the relationships between different frequency components of an audio signal.

Technical Deep Dive

Coherence analysis relies on the concept of spectral coherence, which measures the correlation between two or more signals at different frequencies. By analyzing the coherence between the input audio signal and a reference noise signal, we can identify the frequency components that contribute to noise and echo effects.
