The Problem

EEG signals are a vital tool for diagnosing neurological disorders, monitoring brain activity during surgery, and even tracking patient outcomes after treatment. However, these signals often contain artifacts, noise, and low-frequency components that can obscure valuable information. Current signal processing methods rely on traditional techniques such as filtering, transformation, or independent component analysis (ICA), which may not be effective in removing all types of noise.

To address this challenge, researchers have turned to machine learning algorithms, particularly deep neural networks (DNNs) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs). While these approaches have shown promise, they often require extensive computational resources, large datasets, and meticulous tuning. Moreover, the interpretability of these models can be limited, making it difficult for clinicians to understand the underlying mechanisms.

Technical Deep Dive

Our solution leverages the power of Clisonix's Tide Engine, a distributed computing framework that ensures consistent state across multiple nodes in a healthcare network. This allows us to process EEG signals in real-time, leveraging the collective processing capacity of our infrastructure. Signal Fabric, another key component, weaves together EEG, audio, and biosensor streams into a unified data stream. By doing so, it enables the extraction of features that might be missed by traditional signal processing methods.

We employ a novel combination of techniques to enhance EEG signals:

1. Wavelet Denoising: We apply wavelet transform-based denoising to remove high-frequency noise from the EEG signals. 2. ICA-Based Artifact Removal: Independent Component Analysis (ICA) is used to identify and separate artifacts from the raw EEG data. 3. Deep Learning-Based Filtering: A deep neural network architecture is employed for adaptive filtering, allowing it to learn the optimal filter parameters in real-time.
