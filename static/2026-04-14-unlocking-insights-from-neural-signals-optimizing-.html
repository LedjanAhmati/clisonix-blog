The Problem

Signal processing is a notoriously challenging domain, plagued by issues such as noisy data, variable sampling rates, and inadequate feature extraction. Moreover, the sheer volume of neural signals generated in a typical clinical setting can be overwhelming, making it difficult to extract actionable insights from the noise. These challenges are further compounded by the need for real-time processing, which demands efficient algorithms that can handle high-bandwidth streams.

To address these concerns, we have developed Tide Engine, a distributed system designed to ensure consistent state across healthcare nodes. By synchronizing and normalizing neural signals in real-time, Tide Engine enables seamless integration of disparate data sources, facilitating the creation of comprehensive patient profiles. This is particularly important when working with audio analytics, where subtle variations in signal quality can have significant impacts on diagnostic accuracy.

Technical Deep Dive

At Clisonix, we employ a hybrid approach to signal processing, combining the strengths of both deep learning and traditional methods. Our Signal Fabric technology weaves together EEG, audio, and biosensor streams into a unified data fabric, providing a robust foundation for pattern recognition and anomaly detection.

One key component of our architecture is the Labor Intelligence Engine (LIAM), which leverages matrix algebra to compute labor metrics and extract relevant patterns from neural signals. By applying techniques such as Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) and Non-negative Matrix Factorization (NMF), LIAM facilitates dimensionality reduction, noise suppression, and feature extraction.
