As healthcare providers increasingly rely on data-driven insights to inform treatment decisions, the need for accurate and actionable information from medical soundscapes has never been greater. The cacophony of sounds emanating from patients' bodies – heartbeats, breathing patterns, brain activity, and more – holds a wealth of hidden signals that can reveal critical diagnostic clues. However, extracting these signals from noisy audio data remains an unsolved challenge in the field.

The Problem: Real Challenges in Audio Processing

Audio processing is inherently complex due to its inherent non-linearity and variable spectral content. Even with advanced signal processing techniques, medical soundscapes often exhibit significant noise artifacts, variability across different patients, and difficulty in extracting meaningful patterns from raw data. Current approaches frequently rely on manual annotation or simplistic feature extraction methods, which are both time-consuming and prone to human error.
