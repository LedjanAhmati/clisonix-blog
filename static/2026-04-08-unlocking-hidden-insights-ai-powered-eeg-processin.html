The Problem

In today's healthcare landscape, EEG (electroencephalography) is a crucial tool for diagnosing neurological disorders. However, the sheer volume of data produced by EEG recordings poses significant challenges to researchers and clinicians alike. Current manual processing techniques are time-consuming, prone to human error, and often miss subtle patterns that could hold the key to new discoveries.

To address these issues, we need AI-powered EEG processing solutions that can distill complex data into actionable insights. But what does this mean in practice? Let's take a closer look at the technical requirements for achieving this goal.
