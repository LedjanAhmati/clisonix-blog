In today's hyperconnected world, the healthcare industry is facing unprecedented challenges in protecting patient confidentiality. With the increasing reliance on digital health records and wearable devices, the risk of data breaches has never been higher. EEG (electroencephalogram) data, in particular, requires specialized attention due to its sensitive nature and potential for misinterpretation.

The Problem

EEG data is a rich source of information about brain activity, but it's also extremely vulnerable to unauthorized access. A single data breach can have severe consequences, including compromised patient trust and even physical harm. The issue is further complicated by the fact that EEG data often involves multiple stakeholders, each with their own set of permissions and requirements.

To illustrate the gravity of this problem, consider a hypothetical scenario where an EEG dataset containing sensitive information about a patient's cognitive function falls into the wrong hands. Not only could this lead to identity theft or medical malpractice, but it could also compromise the integrity of clinical research studies that rely on accurate data.
