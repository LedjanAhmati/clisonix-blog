The Problem: Challenges in Data Privacy

Healthcare organizations handle vast amounts of sensitive data, including medical records, genomic information, and wearable device telemetry. With the advent of AI, this data is being increasingly used to train machine learning models that can provide personalized care recommendations. However, these models rely on accurate and reliable data, which often requires sharing or storing patient information in non-secure environments.

The problem lies not only in the technical aspects but also in the regulatory landscape. The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) sets stringent guidelines for safeguarding protected health information (PHI). Moreover, new regulations like the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) in Europe have introduced stricter rules on data anonymization and consent.
