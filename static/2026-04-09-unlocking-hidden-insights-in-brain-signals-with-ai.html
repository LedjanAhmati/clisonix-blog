With the increasing adoption of electroencephalography (EEG), functional near-infrared spectroscopy (fNIRS), and other neuroimaging techniques, clinicians and researchers face a daunting challenge: extracting meaningful insights from vast amounts of noisy, complex data.

The Problem

Signal processing is an inherently challenging task due to its intrinsic complexity. Brain signals are inherently high-dimensional, with multiple frequency bands, time-domain variations, and spatial correlations contributing to their rich structure. Traditional signal processing techniques often fail to capture these nuances, resulting in incomplete or misleading interpretations of brain activity.

Moreover, as the volume and diversity of data grow, so do the demands on computational resources and processing power. Inefficient algorithms can lead to impractical processing times, limiting the applicability of these techniques in real-world settings.
