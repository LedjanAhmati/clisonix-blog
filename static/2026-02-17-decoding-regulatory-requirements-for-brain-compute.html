The Problem

Regulatory compliance for BCI devices poses significant challenges. The ever-changing landscape of medical device regulations and standards can be overwhelming, even for experienced professionals. The FDA's guidance on BCI devices is limited, leaving a gap in clear instructions for developers and manufacturers. Moreover, the complexity of integrating multiple stakeholders, including clinicians, patients, and regulatory bodies, adds to the compliance burden.

To illustrate this challenge, consider the following real-world scenario: a BCI device developer must ensure their product meets the requirements outlined in the FDA's 510(k) clearance process while also adhering to international standards like IEC 60601-1. This involves navigating multiple regulations and guidelines, including those related to electromagnetic compatibility (EMC), radio-frequency interference (RFI), and software validation.
