The Problem: Real Challenges in Sustainable Tech

Artificial intelligence has become an integral part of modern life, from healthcare to finance and beyond. However, its growing demand for computing power is straining the planet's resources. A study by the International Energy Agency (IEA) estimated that AI-powered data centers consume around 1% of global electricity production, with this number projected to triple by 2030. Furthermore, e-waste from discarded hardware and software is becoming a significant environmental concern.

To make matters worse, most current ML models are optimized for performance rather than efficiency. They rely on large amounts of data, complex algorithms, and powerful hardware, resulting in exorbitant energy consumption. As we continue to scale up our AI infrastructure, it's imperative that we address these issues head-on.
