The Problem

Regulatory compliance has always been a critical concern for financial institutions. However, the EU AI Act introduces new challenges that demand a more sophisticated approach. The regulation requires organizations to demonstrate transparency and explainability in their AI decision-making processes, which is easier said than done. Take, for instance, the use of credit risk assessment models, where complex algorithms make decisions based on vast amounts of data. Ensuring that these models are fair, transparent, and compliant with the EU AI Act is a daunting task.

The EU AI Act also introduces new requirements around data protection, emphasizing the need for secure processing and storage of sensitive information. This is particularly relevant for financial institutions, which handle vast amounts of personal and financial data. The regulation's emphasis on human oversight and accountability adds another layer of complexity, requiring organizations to establish clear lines of responsibility and implement robust audit trails.
