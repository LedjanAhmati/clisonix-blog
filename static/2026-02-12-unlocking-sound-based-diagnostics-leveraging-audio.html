The Problem

Audio processing in healthcare has long been plagued by challenges such as noise cancellation, variable signal quality, and data interpretation. Traditional methods often rely on manual analysis or simple threshold-based algorithms, which can lead to inaccurate diagnoses and delayed treatment. Moreover, the sheer volume of audio data generated in medical settings creates a significant barrier to efficient processing and interpretation.
