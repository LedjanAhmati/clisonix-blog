The Problem: Real Challenges in Audio Processing

Audio signals are inherently noisy and variable, making them difficult to analyze using traditional methods. Issues like time-frequency resolution trade-offs, non-stationarity, and spectral estimation limitations plague audio processing pipelines. These challenges become even more pronounced when dealing with real-world data from medical devices, such as EEG recordings or audio-based diagnostic tools.

Furthermore, the sheer volume of audio data generated in healthcare settings can be overwhelming, making it challenging to identify patterns and insights. The need for accurate and efficient signal processing has never been more pressing.
