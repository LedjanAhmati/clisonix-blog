The Problem

Electroencephalography (EEG) signals are a rich source of information about brain activity. However, their analysis poses significant technical challenges:

1. Signal noise: EEG signals are prone to interference from electrical artifacts and sensor limitations. 2. High dimensionality: Raw EEG data contains tens of thousands of channels, making it difficult to extract meaningful insights. 3. Temporal dependencies: Brainwave patterns exhibit complex temporal relationships, requiring sophisticated modeling techniques.

Traditional approaches often rely on manual processing, which is time-consuming and susceptible to human bias. Moreover, these methods frequently sacrifice accuracy for computational efficiency or vice versa.
