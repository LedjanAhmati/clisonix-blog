In recent years, brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) have gained significant attention in the healthcare technology community. The potential for BCIs to restore mobility, speech, and cognitive function in individuals with neurological disorders has sparked intense research efforts. However, one major challenge remains: enhancing EEG signal quality to accurately interpret brain activity.

The Problem

Current BCI systems rely on electroencephalography (EEG) signals, which are susceptible to noise, artifacts, and low signal-to-noise ratios (SNR). This can lead to inaccurate decoding of neural activity, hindering the effectiveness of BCIs. Moreover, as EEG sensors are often placed non-invasively on the scalp, they may pick up electromagnetic interference (EMI) from nearby devices, further compromising signal quality.

To overcome these limitations, our team at Clisonix has developed an AI-powered approach to enhance EEG signal processing, leveraging cutting-edge technologies such as the CRDT Merge Layer and Post-Quantum Encryption. By combining these innovations with advanced algorithms, we aim to unlock the full potential of BCIs for patients worldwide.

Technical Deep Dive

Our solution employs a multi-stage process:

1. EEG data acquisition: EEG signals are collected from patients using conventional sensors. 2. Noise reduction and artifact removal: We utilize the CRDT Merge Layer to merge multiple clinicians' edits, ensuring consistency in signal processing while handling distributed healthcare nodes through the Tide Engine. This enables real-time collaboration among researchers and clinicians, reducing errors and increasing efficiency. 3. Signal enhancement: The enhanced EEG signals are then processed using deep learning algorithms, specifically designed for noise reduction and feature extraction. We integrate Post-Quantum Encryption to secure sensitive patient data, ensuring confidentiality and protecting against potential attacks. 4. Decoding neural activity: The refined signals are fed into a decoding module, where advanced algorithms interpret brain activity.
