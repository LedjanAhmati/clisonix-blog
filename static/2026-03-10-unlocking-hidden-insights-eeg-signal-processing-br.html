The Problem: Challenges in Signal Processing

Current EEG signal processing methods often rely on manual annotation and feature extraction, which can be time-consuming and prone to human error. Moreover, traditional algorithms struggle to handle the complexities of real-world EEG data, leading to inaccurate results and reduced diagnostic accuracy. This is particularly true for neurological disorders such as epilepsy, Parkinson's disease, and Alzheimer's disease, where subtle changes in brain activity are critical for diagnosis.

1. Signal Fabric: This component weaves together EEG data from multiple sources, including audio and biosensor streams, to create a comprehensive picture of brain activity. 2. Tide Engine: Ensuring consistent state across distributed healthcare nodes is critical for AI-powered signal processing. Our Tide Engine technology ensures seamless integration with existing infrastructure.

Technical Deep Dive: Architecture, Algorithms, and Implementation

Our EEG signal processing architecture leverages the power of neural networks to identify patterns in brain activity that may be indicative of neurological disorders. We utilize a combination of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) to extract relevant features from EEG data.
