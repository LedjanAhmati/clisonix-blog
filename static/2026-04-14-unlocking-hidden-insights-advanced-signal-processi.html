The Problem: Real Challenges in Signal Processing

Traditional signal processing methods often struggle with the sheer volume and complexity of medical data generated from various sources, including EEG, audio, and biosensors. Moreover, these techniques often rely on simplistic assumptions about data distributions, which can lead to inaccurate results when dealing with real-world noise and variability.

The limitations of traditional signal processing are evident in several critical areas:

1. Inadequate Handling of Complex Data: Traditional methods fail to effectively handle the intricate relationships between different signals, leading to reduced accuracy and insights. 2. Scalability Issues: Existing techniques often struggle with scaling up to accommodate large datasets and distributed computing environments, hindering their ability to process real-time data. 3. Lack of Adaptability: Conventional methods frequently fail to adapt to changing signal patterns and underlying dynamics, resulting in suboptimal performance.
