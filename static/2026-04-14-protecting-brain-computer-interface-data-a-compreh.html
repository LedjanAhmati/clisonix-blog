As healthcare innovators continue to push the boundaries of what is possible with brain-computer interfaces (BCIs), ensuring the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of sensitive patient data has never been more crucial. The growing use of BCIs in clinical settings and research environments raises significant concerns about data privacy, particularly when it comes to signal processing and analysis.

The Problem

Recent advancements in BCI technology have led to increased adoption, but with this growth comes new challenges. Modern BCIs rely on complex algorithms and sophisticated hardware to decode neural signals, making them vulnerable to cyber threats. The integration of multiple sensors and devices further compounds these risks. A recent study revealed that 75% of healthcare organizations experienced a data breach in the past two years alone.

Moreover, the sensitive nature of BCI data demands rigorous protection against unauthorized access, tampering, or data loss. Regulatory bodies such as HIPAA and GDPR have implemented strict guidelines for handling protected health information (PHI), underscoring the importance of robust security measures.
