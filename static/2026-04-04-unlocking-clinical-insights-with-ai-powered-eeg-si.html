The Problem

Clinical insights are often hindered by the complexity of EEG signals, which can be noisy, non-linear, and difficult to interpret. Current analysis methods rely on manual processing, which is time-consuming and prone to human error. Moreover, the sheer volume of data generated by modern healthcare systems makes it challenging for clinicians to identify meaningful patterns.

To address this problem, we need AI-powered EEG signal analysis that can accurately detect subtle changes in brain activity, allowing for early diagnosis and intervention. However, existing solutions often suffer from high latency, limited scalability, and poor interpretability, hindering their adoption in clinical settings.
