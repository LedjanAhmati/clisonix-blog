The Problem

Signal processing is a daunting task, especially when it comes to EEG data. The sheer volume and complexity of neural activity make it challenging for even the most advanced algorithms to extract meaningful information. Current approaches often rely on oversimplification or neglect the nuances of individual brain patterns, leading to suboptimal results.

Moreover, many existing solutions focus solely on data collection, ignoring the critical aspect of processing and interpretation. This oversight can lead to misdiagnosis, incorrect treatment plans, and decreased patient satisfaction. It's time for a paradigm shift in signal processing – one that prioritizes accuracy, nuance, and actionable insights.
