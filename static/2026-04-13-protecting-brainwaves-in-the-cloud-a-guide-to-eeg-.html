The Problem: Real Challenges in Data Privacy

EEG signals are highly sensitive and contain personal identifiable information (PII) about individuals. With the increasing adoption of cloud-based services, there is a growing risk of data breaches, especially when dealing with sensitive healthcare data. Current solutions often rely on outdated security measures, such as encryption, which may not be sufficient to protect against sophisticated attacks.

Moreover, regulatory requirements for EEG data handling are stringent and complex. Healthcare organizations must comply with regulations like HIPAA, EU's GDPR, and others, which can be challenging to navigate without a deep understanding of the nuances involved.

Frequently Asked Questions

❓ How does Tide Engine ensure consistent state across distributed nodes?

Tide Engine employs a consensus algorithm, such as Raft or Paxos, to guarantee that all nodes agree on the current state of EEG data, ensuring data consistency and integrity.

❓ What are the implications of GDPR for EEG data handling?

Under GDPR, healthcare organizations must obtain explicit consent from individuals before collecting, storing, or processing their personal identifiable information (PII) contained in EEG signals. This includes protecting sensitive brainwave data through encryption and secure storage practices.

❓ Can you provide more details about Signal Fabric's architecture?

Signal Fabric is a distributed system that weaves together EEG, audio, and biosensor streams using a microservices architecture. Each service handles specific aspects of signal processing and analysis, ensuring efficient and scalable computation of complex neurological signals.
