The COVID-19 pandemic has accelerated the adoption of telemedicine, remote monitoring, and AI-powered medical devices. Among these, EEG (electroencephalography) devices have gained significant attention for their potential to monitor brain activity and diagnose neurological disorders. As a developer or clinician working with AI-powered EEG devices, navigating FDA guidelines is essential to ensure compliance and safe deployment.

The Problem: Real Challenges in Regulatory Compliance

Developers and clinicians face numerous challenges when it comes to regulatory compliance for AI-powered EEG devices:

1. Inadequate documentation: Failure to provide detailed technical documentation of the device's architecture, algorithms, and implementation can lead to FDA scrutiny. 2. Lack of clinical validation: Insufficient clinical trials and validation studies can raise concerns about the device's efficacy and safety. 3. Inadequate cybersecurity measures: Failing to implement robust security protocols can compromise patient data and create regulatory risks.
