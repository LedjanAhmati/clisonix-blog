As we continue to navigate the complexities of modern medicine, the importance of accurate and timely decision-making has never been more pronounced. The sheer volume of data generated by patients, medical devices, and healthcare professionals poses significant challenges for clinicians and researchers alike. One often-overlooked yet crucial aspect of this data is audio: from heartbeats and breathing patterns to speech and ambient noise.

The Problem

Audio processing in healthcare is a double-edged sword. On one hand, it offers unparalleled opportunities for diagnosing conditions, monitoring patient well-being, and understanding the intricacies of human physiology. However, on the other hand, manual analysis can be time-consuming, prone to errors, and insensitive to subtle changes.

Take, for instance, the use of EEG (electroencephalography) recordings in neurological assessments. While these signals contain valuable information about brain activity, accurately interpreting their nuances requires a high degree of expertise and computational resources. Even with specialized equipment, manual analysis can be tedious and may lead to missed diagnoses or misinterpretations.
