The Problem: Challenges in Signal Processing

Signal processing is a notoriously challenging domain, even with the most advanced tools. Traditional methods often rely on manual feature extraction, which can be time-consuming and prone to human error. Moreover, many signal processing algorithms are not designed to handle the complexities of real-world EEG data, such as non-stationarity, noise, and artifacts.

Furthermore, modern EEG datasets often consist of multiple streams of data from various sensors, including audio and biosensors. Weaving together these disparate signals into a coherent narrative requires sophisticated techniques that can handle the sheer volume and complexity of data.
