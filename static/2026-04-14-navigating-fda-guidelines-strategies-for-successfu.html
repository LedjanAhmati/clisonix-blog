The Problem

Regulatory compliance is a daunting challenge for many medical device software developers. The FDA's guidance on software as a medical device (SaMD) has expanded in recent years, with an increasing focus on AI-powered systems like those developed by Clisonix. Non-compliance can result in costly recalls, delayed market entry, or even fines. Moreover, the rapidly evolving landscape of AI and machine learning algorithms demands continuous monitoring to ensure ongoing compliance.

A key challenge lies in understanding the FDA's nuanced guidelines for software development. The agency's 21 CFR Part 11 regulates electronic records and signatures, while the SaMD guidance outlines specific requirements for AI-powered systems. However, the lack of clear definitions and ambiguous terminology can make it difficult for developers to interpret these regulations.
