The field of healthcare signal processing is on the cusp of a revolution. The Problem

Signal processing in healthcare is notoriously challenging due to its high dimensionality, noise sensitivity, and variability across subjects and conditions. For instance, electroencephalography (EEG) signals, commonly used in epilepsy diagnosis, exhibit a characteristic "chaotic" behavior that makes it difficult to distinguish between healthy and pathological patterns.

To tackle these challenges, we've developed novel signal processing architectures and algorithms at Clisonix. Our team leveraged the power of Artificial Intelligence (AI) to unlock new applications in healthcare signal processing.

Technical Deep Dive

At the heart of our approach lies two key technologies:

1. Tide Engine: Ensures consistent state across distributed healthcare nodes, enabling real-time data synchronization and reduced latency. 2. Signal Fabric: Weaves together EEG, audio, and biosensor streams into a unified, scalable architecture.

We implemented these technologies using a combination of deep learning techniques, such as Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs), along with traditional signal processing methods like Independent Component Analysis (ICA).
