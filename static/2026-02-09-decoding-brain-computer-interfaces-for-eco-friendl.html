The Problem

Current health monitoring systems rely heavily on resource-intensive infrastructure, generating copious amounts of electronic waste and contributing to greenhouse gas emissions. Moreover, these systems often require invasive procedures, such as implantable devices or cumbersome electrodes, which can be uncomfortable for patients and even lead to complications. Furthermore, the sheer volume of data generated by these systems poses significant storage and processing challenges.

Technical Deep Dive

To address these challenges, we propose a novel architecture that integrates Clisonix's Tide Engine and Signal Fabric technologies with BCI principles. The Tide Engine ensures consistent state across distributed healthcare nodes, guaranteeing seamless communication between devices and minimizing the need for centralized infrastructure. Meanwhile, the Signal Fabric weaves together EEG, audio, and biosensor streams, providing a comprehensive understanding of a patient's physiological state.

Our system employs a hierarchical processing framework, where raw data from BCI sensors is fed into a neural network-based signal processor. This processor extracts relevant features and patterns, which are then transmitted to the Tide Engine for aggregation and analysis. The Signal Fabric plays a crucial role in this process by providing real-time feedback loops, allowing our system to adapt to changing physiological conditions.
