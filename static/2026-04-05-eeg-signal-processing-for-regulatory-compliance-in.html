The Problem

EEG signal processing is a critical component of many medical devices, including those used in neurology, cardiology, and sleep medicine. However, the nuances of EEG data analysis can lead to errors in interpretation, which may result in adverse events or incorrect diagnoses. Moreover, regulatory bodies like the FDA require that medical device software meet strict standards for safety and efficacy.

One major challenge is ensuring the accuracy and reliability of EEG signal processing algorithms. These algorithms must be able to detect subtle patterns in brain activity while minimizing false positives and negatives. Furthermore, developers must also ensure that their software meets the requirements for security, performance, and user experience.
