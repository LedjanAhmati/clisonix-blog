In recent years, we've witnessed unprecedented advancements in artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML). These innovations have transformed various industries, including healthcare. However, despite the significant progress made, one area remains particularly challenging: brain-computer interfaces (BCIs). BCIs rely on electroencephalography (EEG) signals to decode neural activity, but processing these signals in real-time is a daunting task.

The Problem

Current BCI systems struggle with EEG signal processing due to several reasons. First, the sheer volume of data generated by EEG sensors makes it difficult to extract meaningful information in real-time. Traditional ML algorithms often require extensive computational resources and suffer from high latency, rendering them unsuitable for BCI applications. Moreover, existing solutions often rely on complex, bespoke architectures that are difficult to scale or maintain.
