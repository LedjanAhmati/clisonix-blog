As the European Union's Artificial Intelligence Act (AI Act) comes into force, manufacturers are facing a significant challenge in ensuring their AI systems comply with the new regulations. The AI Act aims to regulate the development and deployment of AI systems, particularly those that involve human data, to ensure safety, security, and transparency.

The Problem

Manufacturers are already struggling to keep up with the evolving regulatory landscape. The EU's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) has been in effect since 2018, but the new AI Act introduces additional requirements for AI systems, such as:

* Explainability: Manufacturers must ensure that their AI systems can provide transparent and understandable explanations for their decisions. * Bias and fairness: AI systems must be designed to avoid biases and ensure fair treatment of individuals. * Human oversight: Manufacturers must implement mechanisms for human review and intervention in AI decision-making processes.

Meeting these requirements is a complex task, particularly when it comes to ensuring the explainability and fairness of AI systems. This is where Clisonix's cutting-edge technologies come into play.
