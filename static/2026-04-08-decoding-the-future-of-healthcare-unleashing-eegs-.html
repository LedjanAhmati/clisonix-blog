As healthcare continues to evolve, the importance of accurate and reliable diagnosis cannot be overstated. With the increasing adoption of non-invasive brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) and electroencephalography (EEG)-based systems, the potential for EEG to revolutionize patient care is vast. However, traditional signal processing techniques often fall short in extracting meaningful insights from EEG data, leading to suboptimal outcomes.

The Problem

Current signal processing methods rely on oversimplified approaches that neglect the complexity and intricacies of brain activity patterns. These limitations are exemplified by:

* Time-domain analysis: Focusing solely on amplitude and frequency characteristics fails to capture the intricate relationships between different frequency bands. * Spectral analysis: Using Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) or similar techniques often results in poor time-frequency resolution, missing subtle changes in brain activity. * Manual feature extraction: The human analyst's subjective interpretation of EEG signals can lead to inconsistent and unreliable findings.

Deep Learning-based Feature Extraction: Utilizing convolutional neural networks (CNNs) or recurrent neural networks (RNNs), we extract meaningful features from EEG signals, bypassing the need for manual intervention. 3. Real-time Processing: Leverage the Tide Engine's distributed architecture to process EEG data in real-time, ensuring timely and actionable insights.
