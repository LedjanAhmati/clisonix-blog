The Problem

EEG signal processing has been a cornerstone in neuroscience research for decades. However, traditional methods often fall short when dealing with the intricacies of neural activity, resulting in noisy, inaccurate, or incomplete data. The problem lies in the sheer complexity of brain signals – they are non-stationary, non-linear, and exhibit high dimensionality. This makes it challenging to extract meaningful insights from EEG recordings.

To overcome these limitations, researchers have resorted to various techniques such as wavelet denoising, independent component analysis (ICA), and deep learning-based approaches. While these methods have shown promise, they often require extensive expertise, computational resources, and time-consuming manual annotation.
