The Problem

Despite the rapid advancements in healthcare tech, there are still significant challenges to overcome when it comes to effective patient care. One major hurdle is the sheer volume of data generated by patients with neurological conditions such as epilepsy or Alzheimer's disease. Clinicians struggle to make sense of this data, often relying on manual annotation and interpretation – a time-consuming and error-prone process.

Another critical issue is the need for personalized treatment plans that take into account individual patient characteristics and responses. Current systems often rely on generic algorithms that fail to capture the unique nuances of each patient's condition.

FAQ

Q: What is the CRDT Merge Layer?

The CRDT Merge Layer is a distributed data structure that enables multiple clinicians to edit the same patient data simultaneously, while ensuring data consistency and accuracy.

Q: How does Post-Quantum Encryption work in this context? A: Post-Quantum Encryption ensures the secure transmission of sensitive medical data between nodes, safeguarding against potential quantum computing threats.

Q: What is the Tide Engine? A: The Tide Engine is a state management system that guarantees consistent state across distributed healthcare nodes, ensuring seamless collaboration and accurate patient data.
