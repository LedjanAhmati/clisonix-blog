Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What are the key differences between SaMD and traditional medical devices?

Software as a Medical Device (SaMD) is a category of medical devices that use software to diagnose, monitor, or treat patients. Unlike traditional medical devices, SaMD does not require a physical device to function.

Q: How do I determine which FDA guidelines apply to my product? A: The FDA provides guidance on the classification of SaMD in its guidance document "Software as a Medical Device (SaMD): Clinical Evaluation; Guidance for Industry and Food and Drug Administration Staff." Review this document to determine which guidelines apply to your specific product.

Q: What is the role of validation in regulatory compliance? A: Validation is the process of demonstrating that software solutions meet FDA requirements. This includes developing test plans, conducting testing, and documenting results.

Q: Can I use existing development best practices for my SaMD project? A: Yes, but you must also consider specific guidelines related to SaMD, such as those outlined in IEC 62304 or ISO 13485.
