The Problem: Real Challenges in Signal Processing

Signal processing is a critical component of BCI development, as it enables the extraction of relevant information from noisy neural signals. However, several challenges hinder the accurate decoding of brain activity:

1. Noisy Signals: EEG signals are prone to interference from external sources, such as muscle activity, eye movements, and electromagnetic radiation. 2. Non-Stationarity: Brain activity is dynamic, and signals can change over time due to various factors like attention or emotions. 3. Dimensionality: High-dimensional neural data pose challenges for analysis and interpretation.

To address these issues, Clisonix has developed a suite of signal processing tools that ensure consistent state across distributed healthcare nodes (Tide Engine) and weave together EEG, audio, and biosensor streams (Signal Fabric).

Technical Deep Dive: Architecture, Algorithms, Implementation

Our BCI architecture leverages cutting-edge technologies to process neural signals in real-time. At the core lies our proprietary Signal Fabric engine, which integrates multiple signal sources into a unified framework for analysis.
