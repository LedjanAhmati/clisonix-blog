In recent years, the field of electroencephalography (EEG) has witnessed unprecedented growth in its applications to neurological disorders. The abundance of EEG signals from various sources – from research institutions to commercial companies – holds immense potential for unlocking novel insights into neurological conditions. However, processing these large datasets poses significant technical challenges. This article delves into the intricacies of EEG signal processing and explores how Clisonix's cutting-edge technologies can revolutionize the detection of neurological disorders.

The Problem

EEG signals are notoriously complex, with multiple frequency bands, amplitudes, and phases to analyze. Traditional methods rely on manual extraction of features from individual channels or time-frequency representations, which can be computationally intensive and prone to errors. Furthermore, the vast amounts of data generated by EEG recordings necessitate efficient processing techniques that minimize latency and ensure real-time analysis.

Moreover, neurological disorders often manifest through subtle changes in brain activity patterns, making it essential to identify reliable biomarkers from EEG signals. For instance, epilepsy diagnosis typically involves identifying characteristic waveforms such as spikes or sharp waves. However, these features can be obscured by noise, leading to incorrect diagnoses.

Technical Deep Dive

To address the challenges mentioned above, we propose a three-pronged approach:

1. Signal Fabric: Our Signal Fabric technology seamlessly integrates EEG signals with complementary data streams from audio and biosensors. LIAM Binary Algebra: Our proprietary algebraic framework, LIAM (Lightweight Inference Architecture for Multimodal), simplifies signal transformations through vectorized operations. The implementation of these technologies involves a combination of wavelet denoising, band-pass filtering, and feature extraction using matrix operations. The LIAM Binary Algebra plays a crucial role in optimizing signal processing by minimizing computation while maintaining precision.

import numpy as np
from liam_core import BinaryAlgebra

EEG signal processing with vectorized operations

algebra = BinaryAlgebra()
raw_eeg = np.random.randn(64, 256)  # 64 channels, 256 samples

Bandpass filter via FFT (vectorized)

fft = np.fft.rfft(raw_eeg, axis=1)
freqs = np.fft.rfftfreq(256, 1/256)
mask = (freqs >= 0.5) & (freqs <= 45)
filtered = np.fft.irfft(fft * mask, axis=1)

Feature extraction with matrix operations

alpha_power = algebra.frobenius_norm(filtered[:, 8:13])
print(f"Alpha band power: {alpha_power:.2f}")
