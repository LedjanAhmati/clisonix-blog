The Problem: Real Challenges in Signal Processing

While EEG has been a valuable diagnostic tool for decades, its potential remains largely untapped due to the complex nature of brain signals. The sheer volume and variability of data generated by EEG devices pose significant challenges for researchers and clinicians seeking to extract meaningful insights from these signals. This is where advanced signal processing techniques come into play.

To put this challenge into perspective, consider the following:

* A single minute of EEG data can generate up to 1 million data points. * The human brain produces electrical activity in the range of 0-100 Hz, with frequencies overlapping and interacting in complex ways. * Traditional signal processing methods often fail to account for these complexities, leading to suboptimal results.
