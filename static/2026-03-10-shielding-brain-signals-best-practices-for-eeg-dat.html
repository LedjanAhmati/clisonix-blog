In today's healthcare landscape, the use of Electroencephalography (EEG) has become increasingly prevalent. As a non-invasive method of recording electrical activity in the brain, EEG provides valuable insights into cognitive function, neurological disorders, and more. However, with the increased adoption of EEG comes a critical concern: data protection.

The Problem

As healthcare organizations collect and store sensitive patient data, including EEG signals, they face numerous challenges in ensuring data privacy. The problem is multifaceted:

1. Data Volume: EEG generates vast amounts of raw data, making it difficult to manage and protect. 2. Data Sensitivity: EEG signals are highly sensitive and contain personal identifiable information (PII). 3. Regulatory Compliance: Meeting regulatory requirements for data protection, such as HIPAA in the United States, is a significant concern.
