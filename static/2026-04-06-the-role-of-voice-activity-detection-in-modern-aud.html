In today's healthcare landscape, audio analytics is becoming increasingly crucial for improving patient care and streamlining clinical workflows. With the rise of remote monitoring and telemedicine, medical professionals are relying more heavily on audio data to monitor patients' vital signs, speech patterns, and other critical indicators. However, extracting valuable insights from audio streams requires sophisticated processing techniques – particularly voice activity detection (VAD).

The Problem

Real-time audio analysis is fraught with challenges. Audio signals are inherently noisy and variable, making it difficult to distinguish between relevant and irrelevant information. Furthermore, the sheer volume of data generated by audio streams can overwhelm even the most robust analytics systems. To make matters worse, traditional VAD approaches often rely on simplistic threshold-based methods that fail to account for context-specific variations in speech patterns.
