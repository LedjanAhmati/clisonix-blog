The healthcare industry is facing an unprecedented challenge. The growing demand for medical diagnostics and treatments has put a strain on resources, leading to increased energy consumption and greenhouse gas emissions. In this context, the development of sustainable technologies that can reduce the environmental footprint of medical diagnostics has become imperative.

The Problem

Current medical diagnostic tools rely heavily on non-renewable energy sources and generate significant amounts of electronic waste. The process of interpreting complex biomedical signals from patients is often manual, time-consuming, and prone to human error. Furthermore, traditional methods of data storage and processing result in substantial energy consumption and e-waste generation.

For instance, the use of electroencephalography (EEG) devices in clinical settings requires significant amounts of electricity to power the equipment, which can contribute to greenhouse gas emissions. Moreover, the storage and analysis of EEG data generate substantial electronic waste, which is often not recycled properly.
