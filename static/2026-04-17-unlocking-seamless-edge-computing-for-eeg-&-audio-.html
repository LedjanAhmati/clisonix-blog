The Problem: Real Challenges in Edge Computing

While edge computing offers numerous advantages, such as reduced latency, lower bandwidth requirements, and increased scalability, it also presents several challenges. In the context of EEG and audio analytics in healthcare, some of these challenges include:

* Handling high-bandwidth data streams from multiple sources * Ensuring consistent state across distributed nodes while maintaining low-latency processing * Managing the complexity of edge computing architectures to accommodate diverse hardware configurations

To overcome these challenges, it's essential to have a deep understanding of edge computing fundamentals and to develop solutions that can seamlessly integrate with existing systems.
