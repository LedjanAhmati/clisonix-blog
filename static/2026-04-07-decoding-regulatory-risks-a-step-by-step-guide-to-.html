In the rapidly evolving landscape of healthcare AI, regulatory compliance has become an urgent priority. With the increasing adoption of EEG and audio analytics in medical devices, ensuring adherence to strict regulations is crucial to avoid costly fines, reputational damage, and most importantly, compromised patient safety.

The Problem

Developing compliant medical device software requires navigating a complex web of regulations, including FDA guidelines for mobile medical applications (21 CFR Part 11), EU's Medical Device Regulation (MDR) 2017/745, and IEC 62304:2006. Moreover, the use of AI-powered analytics in medical devices introduces additional challenges related to transparency, explainability, and data governance.

To make matters more complicated, healthcare companies must also contend with the rapidly changing regulatory landscape. The COVID-19 pandemic has accelerated the adoption of remote monitoring and telemedicine solutions, forcing regulators to reassess their approach to digital health technologies.
