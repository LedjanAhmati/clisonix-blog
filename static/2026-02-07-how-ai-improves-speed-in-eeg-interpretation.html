The Problem: Challenges in EEG Neuroscience

EEG is a non-invasive neuroimaging technique that measures electrical activity in the brain. While it has revolutionized our understanding of brain function, interpreting EEG data remains a time-consuming and labor-intensive process. Traditionally, researchers have relied on manual analysis, which can be prone to human error and limited by the sheer volume of data generated by modern EEG systems.

Furthermore, EEG signals are inherently noisy and complex, making it difficult to extract meaningful information without sophisticated processing techniques. Even with state-of-the-art algorithms, interpretation times can range from minutes to hours per dataset, hindering the pace of research and clinical decision-making.
