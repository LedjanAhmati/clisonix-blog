As healthcare providers increasingly adopt AI-powered tools to analyze complex medical signals like EEG (electroencephalogram) recordings, the importance of data governance and patient consent cannot be overstated. The Problem

EEG data contains rich insights into brain function, but it also represents a significant risk for breaches in patient confidentiality. The sheer volume and complexity of EEG signals necessitate sophisticated processing techniques to extract meaningful patterns and correlations. However, the lack of standardization in EEG signal formats, combined with the need for real-time processing, creates a regulatory challenge that healthcare organizations must address.
