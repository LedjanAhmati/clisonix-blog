As the healthcare industry continues to grapple with the challenges of integrating artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) systems, one key issue remains at the forefront: explainability. In an era where medical decisions rely increasingly on AI-driven insights, clinicians must be able to understand not only what these models are doing but also why they're making specific predictions or recommendations.

The Problem

Current AI/ML approaches often struggle with transparency and interpretability, leading to several critical challenges:

1. Lack of trust: Clinicians may question the reliability of AI-driven insights if they don't understand how the model arrived at its conclusions. 2. Regulatory hurdles: Healthcare institutions must comply with strict regulations regarding data privacy and transparency, which can be difficult to navigate without explainable AI. 3. Clinical adoption: If clinicians can't interpret or trust AI-driven results, these systems will never gain widespread acceptance in clinical settings.
