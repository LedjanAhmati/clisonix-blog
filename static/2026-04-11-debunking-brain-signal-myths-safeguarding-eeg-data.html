In today's healthcare landscape, the use of electroencephalography (EEG) and other biosensors has revolutionized the way we diagnose and treat neurological conditions. However, the increasing reliance on EEG data raises critical concerns about data privacy and compliance. Recent breaches have highlighted the need for robust safeguards to protect sensitive patient information.

The Problem

EEG data is inherently vulnerable due to its high sensitivity and specificity. A single misplaced or misinterpreted signal can lead to incorrect diagnoses, compromising patient care. Moreover, the widespread adoption of EEG in various clinical settings has created a complex ecosystem with multiple stakeholders involved in handling and processing this sensitive data.

One major challenge lies in ensuring that EEG data is handled and processed according to regulatory guidelines. Non-compliance can result in severe penalties, damage to reputation, and loss of trust among patients and healthcare professionals alike. Furthermore, the lack of standardized protocols for EEG data management has led to inconsistencies in data quality, accuracy, and security.
