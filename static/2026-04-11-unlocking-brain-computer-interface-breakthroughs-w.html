The field of brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) has been rapidly advancing in recent years, promising to revolutionize the way we interact with technology and potentially transform the lives of individuals with paralysis or other motor disorders. However, one of the major challenges facing BCI researchers is the accurate interpretation of EEG signals, which are often plagued by noise and variability.

The Problem

Current BCI systems rely heavily on manual feature extraction and machine learning algorithms to decode neural activity from EEG signals. This approach is time-consuming, labor-intensive, and prone to errors. Moreover, it requires a significant amount of data for training and validation, which can be difficult to obtain in clinical settings. The lack of robustness and scalability has hindered the widespread adoption of BCIs in healthcare.

Technical Deep Dive

Our platform utilizes a novel architecture that integrates multiple machine learning algorithms for feature extraction, denoising, and decoding of EEG signals. The system consists of three main components:

Decoding: We utilize the CRDT Merge Layer to enable multiple clinicians to edit patient data simultaneously while ensuring data consistency.
