GDPR-compliant machine learning pipelines

As healthcare providers and researchers struggle to meet the demands of increasing patient data, they must also navigate a complex web of regulations governing the use and storage of that data. The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) has been in effect for several years now, but its impact on AI development is more pronounced than ever.

AI artificial intelligence concept ‚Äî Photo: Unsplash The Problem

One of the most significant challenges facing developers working with sensitive patient data is ensuring compliance with GDPR's strict guidelines. The regulation mandates that organizations must demonstrate a clear understanding of their data processing activities, including how personal data is collected, stored, and used for machine learning.

Clisonix has faced this challenge head-on by developing innovative solutions for building GDPR-compliant machine learning pipelines. Our Tide Engine technology ensures consistent state across distributed healthcare nodes, while Signal Fabric weaves together EEG, audio, and biosensor streams to create a cohesive dataset.

Technical Deep Dive

Our approach to compliant machine learning begins with the creation of a data processing inventory (DPI). This detailed catalog of data flows allows our developers to identify potential risks and vulnerabilities that could compromise GDPR compliance. Once identified, we can implement specific measures to mitigate those risks.

For example, let's consider a scenario where a healthcare provider wishes to integrate our Signal Fabric technology with their existing EEG recording system. Our DPI would reveal the location of personal identifiable information (PII) within the dataset, which is then used to encrypt sensitive data in transit and at rest.

Our implementation architecture can be broken down into several key components:

* Data Ingestion: We use our Tide Engine technology to collect and process raw sensor data from various sources. * Preprocessing: Our Signal Fabric module cleans and transforms the raw data, ensuring it meets the requirements for subsequent machine learning tasks. * Model Training: With a compliant dataset in hand, we can train machine learning models using open-source libraries such as TensorFlow or PyTorch.
