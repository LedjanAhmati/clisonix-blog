The Problem

Signal processing is a fundamental aspect of EEG analysis, yet it poses significant challenges due to the inherent complexity and variability of brain activity patterns. Raw EEG data often contains:

1. Noise: Electromagnetic interference from external sources, muscle artifacts, and other biological signals. 2. Artifacts: Eye movements, blinks, and other involuntary muscle contractions that corrupt the signal. 3. Frequency content: Variability in power spectral density across different frequency bands (e.g., alpha, beta, theta).

To overcome these challenges, researchers have employed various techniques, including filtering, decomposition, and machine learning-based approaches. However, most methods are either limited in their ability to generalize or require extensive manual tuning.
