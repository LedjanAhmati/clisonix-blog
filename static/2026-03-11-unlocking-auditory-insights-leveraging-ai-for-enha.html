The Problem

Audio signals from various medical devices, such as ECGs (electrocardiograms), auscultation recordings, and speech recognition systems, contain valuable information that often goes underutilized. Current methods rely on manual analysis or simplistic algorithms that fail to capture the complexities of human physiology. This limitation can lead to delayed diagnoses, misinterpretations, and even unnecessary procedures.

One challenge lies in processing the sheer volume of audio data generated by these medical devices. With the proliferation of IoT-enabled healthcare systems, clinicians face an overwhelming influx of data from diverse sources. Traditional signal processing techniques often struggle to keep pace with this data deluge, leading to inaccuracies and missed opportunities for early intervention.
