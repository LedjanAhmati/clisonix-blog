The Problem: Real Challenges in Audio Processing

Audio processing techniques, such as spectral analysis and time-frequency representation, are widely used in various applications, including music and speech recognition. However, when it comes to EEG signals, these methods often fail to capture subtle changes in brain activity patterns due to their high temporal resolution and complexity.

Furthermore, the sheer volume of data generated by modern healthcare systems poses a significant challenge for real-time analysis. Existing solutions often rely on manual processing or simple algorithms that cannot cope with the intricacies of EEG signals.
