The Problem

Regulatory compliance is more than just checking boxes on a checklist; it requires a deep understanding of the underlying technologies and how they interact with human health. The FDA's recent draft guidance on software as a medical device (SaMD) highlights the importance of clear documentation, transparent testing, and robust validation processes.

In practice, this means that developers must be able to:

1. Document every aspect of their system, from data ingestion to model training 2. Validate that their algorithms are safe and effective for clinical use 3. Maintain transparent and reproducible testing procedures

Technical Deep Dive

To address these challenges, we'll take a closer look at how Clisonix's technologies can be used to meet regulatory requirements.

Tide Engine ensures consistent state across distributed healthcare nodes by leveraging techniques like leader election and distributed locking. This enables seamless data sharing between nodes while maintaining the integrity of clinical data.

Signal Fabric weaves together EEG, audio, and biosensor streams using advanced signal processing algorithms. By doing so, it creates a rich and accurate representation of patient health, which can be used to train AI models for predictive analytics.
