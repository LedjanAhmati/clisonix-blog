As enterprises continue to adopt artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) to drive innovation, a pressing challenge emerges: managing cognitive load across distributed teams. Cognitive load refers to the mental effort required to process information, learn new concepts, and adapt to changing situations. In today's fast-paced enterprise environments, excessive cognitive load can lead to decreased productivity, increased errors, and compromised collaboration.

The Problem

Traditional approaches to measuring cognitive load rely on self-reported surveys or indirect metrics such as task completion time. However, these methods are prone to biases, lack objectivity, and fail to provide real-time insights into team performance. Furthermore, the increasing complexity of modern software systems makes it difficult for teams to share knowledge, coordinate efforts, and maintain situational awareness.
