The Problem: Real Challenges in Data Privacy

As healthcare organizations continue to integrate EEG and other biosensor technologies into their workflows, they must also address the inherent risks associated with these systems. These challenges include:

* Data transmission: EEG signals are transmitted wirelessly or via cables, making them susceptible to interception and tampering. * Storage and processing: Sensitive neurological data requires robust storage and processing solutions to prevent unauthorized access and data corruption. * Interoperability: Integrating EEG data with existing healthcare systems can create vulnerabilities due to differences in data formats and protocols.

These challenges underscore the need for a comprehensive approach to EEG data security, one that prioritizes both technical expertise and human understanding of brain wave patterns.
