The Problem: Real Challenges in Healthcare Tech

In recent years, we've seen a surge in interest around brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) and neurophysiological signal processing. However, existing solutions often rely on static templates or one-dimensional feedback mechanisms, neglecting the rich complexities of human perception. Moreover, the sheer volume of data generated by EEG sensors can be daunting to process and analyze.

To make matters worse, current clinical workflows often involve multiple clinicians editing patient data simultaneously, leading to potential conflicts and inconsistencies in diagnoses and treatment plans. Securely integrating sensitive medical information while ensuring future-proof security is another significant challenge.
