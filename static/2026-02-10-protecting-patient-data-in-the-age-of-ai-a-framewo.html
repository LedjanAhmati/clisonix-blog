The Problem: EEG Signal Integrity

EEG signals are a critical component of various neuroscientific and clinical applications, including diagnosing neurological disorders, monitoring anesthesia levels, and assessing brain-computer interface capabilities. These delicate signals require meticulous handling to maintain their integrity. Unfortunately, as we increasingly rely on AI-driven systems for data processing and analysis, the risk of signal degradation or unauthorized access becomes a pressing concern.

EEG data is particularly vulnerable due to its complex temporal and spatial characteristics. Any disruption in the signal chain can compromise patient confidentiality, leading to severe consequences, including misdiagnosis, incorrect treatment plans, or even patient harm. Furthermore, with the growing adoption of AI-assisted clinical decision support systems (CDSSs), there's a heightened risk of data breaches, where sensitive information could be leaked or manipulated.
