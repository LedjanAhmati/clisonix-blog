As of January 2024, the European Union's Artificial Intelligence (AI) Act has come into effect, significantly impacting how financial institutions approach AI development and deployment. With increasing scrutiny on AI-driven decisions, institutions must now adhere to strict guidelines on transparency, accountability, and human oversight.

The Problem

Financial institutions have long faced challenges in regulatory compliance, particularly when integrating AI systems into their operations. The EU AI Act introduces new obligations for organizations handling high-risk AI applications, such as those involving credit scoring or risk assessment. Compliance requires an unprecedented level of transparency, explainability, and auditability, which can be daunting to achieve.
