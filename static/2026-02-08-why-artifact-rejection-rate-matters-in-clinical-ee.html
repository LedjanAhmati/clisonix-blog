The Problem Artifacts in EEG data arise from various sources: electrical interference (EMI), muscle activity, eye movement, or even the subject's own neural activity. These anomalies can render entire datasets unreliable, leading researchers to question their findings and clinicians to misdiagnose patients. In fact, a study published in the Journal of Clinical Neurophysiology estimated that up to 20% of EEG recordings are contaminated by artifacts.
