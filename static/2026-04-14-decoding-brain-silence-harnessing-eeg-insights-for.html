The Problem

Traditional approaches to energy management in hospitals often rely on outdated methods, such as static occupancy sensors or manual adjustments of lighting and HVAC systems. However, these strategies fail to account for the dynamic needs of patients and staff, resulting in wasted resources and unnecessary emissions. Furthermore, the increasing complexity of healthcare infrastructure demands more sophisticated solutions that can adapt to the unique requirements of each facility.

Technical Deep Dive

To address this challenge, Clisonix has developed a novel system combining EEG data with our Tide Engine's distributed processing capabilities and Signal Fabric's real-time stream weaving. This fusion enables the creation of personalized energy efficiency profiles for individual patients, ensuring optimal comfort while minimizing waste.

At the heart of our solution lies the LIAM Binary Algebra (Labor Intelligence Analytics Model), which employs advanced matrix algebra to analyze EEG patterns and infer cognitive load. This information is then used to adjust lighting, temperature, and even music therapy to match each patient's unique needs.
