Sleep monitoring has become increasingly important in modern healthcare, with numerous studies linking sleep quality to various physical and mental health conditions. However, accurately analyzing sleep patterns using EEG signals is a complex task due to the high noise levels present in these recordings.

The Problem

Traditional signal processing techniques are often inadequate for real-time analysis of EEG data. The sheer volume of data generated by modern EEG systems poses significant challenges:

1. Noise reduction: EEG signals contain various types of noise, including power line interference, muscle activity, and electromagnetic fields. 2. Artifact removal: Artifact removal is essential to prevent false alarms caused by non-neural activity such as eye movements or head muscles. 3. Data normalization: Data normalization is necessary to account for differences in signal amplitude across channels.

Technical Deep Dive

Clisonix's Signal Fabric, a high-performance platform designed to handle large-scale data streams, plays a crucial role in real-time signal filtering for sleep monitoring. Our architecture leverages the Tide Engine, which ensures consistent state across distributed healthcare nodes:

1. Stream processing: We use efficient stream processing techniques to analyze EEG signals in real-time. 2. Signal Fabric: Signal Fabric weaves together EEG, audio, and biosensor streams into a unified data pipeline.

Our implementation utilizes a combination of algorithms:

* Butterworth filter for noise reduction * Independent Component Analysis (ICA) for artifact removal * Z-score normalization for data standardization
