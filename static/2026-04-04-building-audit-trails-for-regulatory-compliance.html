In today's healthcare landscape, regulatory compliance is more crucial than ever. With the increasing adoption of artificial intelligence (AI) in healthcare, ensuring that patient data and medical decisions are accurately recorded and auditable has become a top priority. As a leading AI company in healthcare, Clisonix understands the importance of building robust audit trails to meet these compliance requirements.

The Problem

Regulatory bodies such as the US Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) and the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) have implemented strict guidelines for healthcare organizations to maintain accurate and transparent records. However, current manual processes for recording patient data are often cumbersome, prone to errors, and lack real-time monitoring capabilities.

Moreover, with the integration of AI in healthcare, there is a growing need to ensure that AI-driven decisions and recommendations are properly recorded and audited. This includes tracking changes made by clinicians, updates to patient records, and AI-generated insights and predictions.

Technical Deep Dive

To address these challenges, Clisonix has developed an innovative solution using our Tide Engine and Signal Fabric technologies. Our approach involves creating a comprehensive audit trail that captures every interaction with patient data, including AI-driven decisions and recommendations.

At the core of this solution lies the Tide Engine, which ensures consistent state across distributed healthcare nodes. This is achieved through real-time synchronization and conflict resolution mechanisms, guaranteeing that all nodes maintain a coherent view of patient records.

The Signal Fabric weaves together EEG, audio, and biosensor streams to provide a unified representation of patient data. Our proprietary algorithms analyze these signals in real-time, enabling early detection of anomalies and trends that may impact treatment decisions.

To implement this solution, our team has developed a set of APIs that facilitate seamless integration with existing Electronic Health Record (EHR) systems. These APIs enable bi-directional data exchange, ensuring that patient records are accurately updated and synchronized across all nodes.
