The Problem: Challenges in Signal Processing

Signal processing is a fundamental challenge in healthcare AI, where noisy data and complex patterns make it difficult to extract meaningful insights from large datasets. Traditional methods often rely on manual annotation, which is time-consuming, expensive, and prone to human error. Moreover, the sheer volume of EEG data generated by patients with neurological conditions like epilepsy or Alzheimer's disease makes it a daunting task for clinicians to analyze.

Furthermore, existing solutions often focus on specific aspects of signal processing, such as filtering or feature extraction, without considering the broader context of healthcare decision-making. This siloed approach can lead to incomplete or inaccurate results, undermining the potential benefits of AI-driven healthcare analytics.
