The Problem: Real Challenges in Audio Processing

Cochlear implant sound processing involves converting electrical signals into sound waves that the brain can interpret. However, this process is complex and requires precise control over various factors, including signal-to-noise ratio, spectral balance, and temporal processing. Existing methods often rely on manual tuning and expert adjustments, which are time-consuming, prone to human error, and may not fully capture the nuances of each individual's hearing needs.

Furthermore, traditional audio analytics tools typically focus on surface-level metrics such as SNR (signal-to-noise ratio) or spectral balance, without delving deeper into the intricacies of sound processing. This limited approach fails to account for the complex interactions between different auditory pathways and neural responses.
