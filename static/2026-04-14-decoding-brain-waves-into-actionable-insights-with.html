The Problem: Real Challenges in Signal Processing

Traditional signal processing techniques struggle to keep pace with the complexity of real-world data. EEG signals, in particular, are notorious for their high noise-to-signal ratio, making it challenging to extract meaningful insights from the raw data. Moreover, existing methods often rely on simplistic assumptions about brain wave patterns, which can lead to inaccurate conclusions.

To address these challenges, we need a more sophisticated approach that leverages AI-powered signal processing techniques. This requires a deep understanding of both the underlying neuroscience and the computational frameworks required to analyze the data.
