The Problem

Real-time data analysis is a holy grail for healthcare innovation. With the proliferation of IoT devices and wearable sensors, medical professionals are now faced with an unprecedented amount of data. However, processing this data in near real-time is a daunting task, particularly when it comes to EEG signals.

EEG signals, which measure electrical activity in the brain, are notoriously complex and sensitive to noise. Traditional signal processing methods often rely on centralized cloud infrastructure, which introduces significant latency and reduces the speed of decision-making. Moreover, these methods often require extensive computational resources, making them inaccessible to resource-constrained edge devices.
