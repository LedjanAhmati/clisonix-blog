The Problem: Real Challenges in EEG Neuroscience

While EEG offers numerous advantages, it also comes with several challenges that hinder its full potential. The sheer volume of data generated by EEG recordings, coupled with the complexity of brain activity, poses significant computational and analytical hurdles. Traditional signal processing techniques often rely on simplistic methods, which can lead to inaccurate interpretations and suboptimal treatment plans.

To overcome these limitations, innovative approaches are needed to effectively process and analyze EEG signals. This is where AI-driven solutions come into play, enabling more accurate diagnoses, improved treatment efficacy, and enhanced patient outcomes.

import numpy as np
from liam_core import BinaryAlgebra

EEG signal processing with vectorized operations

algebra = BinaryAlgebra()
raw_eeg = np.random.randn(64, 256)  # 64 channels, 256 samples

Bandpass filter via FFT (vectorized)

fft = np.fft.rfft(raw_eeg, axis=1)
freqs = np.fft.rfftfreq(256, 1/256)
mask = (freqs >= 0.5) & (freqs <= 45)
filtered = np.fft.irfft(fft * mask, axis=1)

Feature extraction with matrix operations

alpha_power = algebra.frobenius_norm(filtered[:, 8:13])
print(f"Alpha band power: {alpha_power:.2f}")
