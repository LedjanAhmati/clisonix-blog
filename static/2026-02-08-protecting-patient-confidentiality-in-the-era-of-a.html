The Problem

EEG (electroencephalogram) analysis is becoming increasingly popular in healthcare due to its non-invasive nature and ability to provide valuable insights into brain activity. However, this increased adoption comes with significant challenges when it comes to protecting patient confidentiality. EEG data is sensitive and can reveal a wealth of personal information about an individual's medical history, cognitive abilities, and even emotional state.

Moreover, the use of AI and ML in analyzing EEG data introduces additional risks. These complex algorithms often rely on large datasets, which can lead to data breaches if not properly secured. Furthermore, the increasing reliance on cloud-based infrastructure for storing and processing sensitive healthcare data creates new vulnerabilities that must be addressed.
