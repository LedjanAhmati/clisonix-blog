The Problem

Real-time processing of brain signals at the edge of the network is a significant challenge in healthcare. Traditional methods rely on transmitting raw data from wearable devices or scalp electrodes to centralized servers for analysis. This approach introduces latency, increases bandwidth requirements, and raises concerns about data privacy. Moreover, the sheer volume of data generated by EEG signals necessitates sophisticated signal processing techniques.

To overcome these challenges, we need a more distributed and efficient approach to edge computing. Clisonix's Tide Engine ensures consistent state across distributed healthcare nodes, enabling seamless communication between devices. Meanwhile, Signal Fabric weaves together EEG, audio, and biosensor streams, providing real-time insights into brain activity.

Technical Deep Dive

Our edge-enabled EEG analytics platform leverages the power of distributed computing to process and analyze brain signals in real-time. The architecture consists of three primary components:

1. Distributed Node: Each node is equipped with a small-scale AI engine, capable of processing and analyzing EEG data independently. 2. Tide Engine: This component ensures consistent state across all nodes, enabling seamless communication and synchronization between devices. 3. Signal Fabric: Weave together EEG, audio, and biosensor streams using our proprietary framework.

The signal processing pipeline employs a combination of spectral filtering, wavelet denoising, and deep learning-based feature extraction. Our implementation utilizes the LIAM (Labor Intelligence Analysis Module) binary algebra engine, which enables efficient computation and pattern recognition in real-time.
