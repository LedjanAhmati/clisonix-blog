The Problem: Real Challenges in Edge Computing

Edge computing is not without its challenges. As medical devices and sensors generate more data than ever before, the need for real-time analysis becomes increasingly pressing. But processing and analyzing this data in real-time at the edge poses significant technical hurdles:

1. Scalability: Healthcare settings require scalable solutions to handle a high volume of devices and patients. 2. Data Variety: The heterogeneity of medical data streams (EEG, audio, biosensors) necessitates adaptable architectures that can accommodate diverse input formats. 3. Latency: Delays in analysis can have serious consequences; AI models must be capable of processing data in near real-time to inform critical decisions.
