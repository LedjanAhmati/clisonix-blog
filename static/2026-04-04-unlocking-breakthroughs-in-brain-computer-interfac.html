As healthcare technology continues to advance at an unprecedented pace, one area that holds significant promise is Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCIs). BCIs have the potential to revolutionize patient care by enabling individuals to interact with devices using only their thoughts. However, implementing effective BCIs requires a deep understanding of EEG signals and sophisticated AI-powered algorithms.

The Problem

Current BCI systems face numerous challenges, including:

1. Signal noise: EEG signals are inherently noisy, making it difficult to accurately interpret brain activity. 2. Limited resolution: Most existing BCIs rely on low-resolution EEG sensors, which can lead to poor signal quality and decreased accuracy. 3. Data management: As BCI systems generate vast amounts of data, managing and processing this information in real-time is a significant challenge.
