EEG (Electroencephalography) devices are widely used in neuroscience research and clinical applications to record electrical activity in the human brain. However, one of the significant challenges in EEG is motion artifact removal ‚Äì unwanted signals generated by movement, muscle contractions, or other external factors that can distort the accuracy of EEG readings.

The Problem

Motion artifacts can be particularly problematic in EEG recordings, especially when working with patients who have mobility issues or are required to perform specific tasks during the recording process. These artifacts can manifest as sudden changes in amplitude, frequency, or phase shifts in the EEG signal, making it challenging to interpret the data accurately.

Current motion artifact removal techniques often rely on manual inspection and processing of raw EEG data, which is both time-consuming and prone to human error. Moreover, traditional methods may not be effective in removing artifacts caused by complex movements or those with high-frequency components.

from liam_core import BinaryAlgebra

EEG signal processing with vectorized operations

algebra = BinaryAlgebra()
raw_eeg = np.random.randn(64, 256)  # 64 channels, 256 samples

Bandpass filter via FFT (vectorized)

fft = np.fft.rfft(raw_eeg, axis=1)
freqs = np.fft.rfftfreq(256, 1/256)
mask = (freqs >= 0.5) & (freqs <= 45)
filtered = np.fft.irfft(fft * mask, axis=1)
