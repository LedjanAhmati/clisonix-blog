Why this matters NOW:

The emergence of non-invasive neurotechnologies has led to a surge in research on the neural mechanisms underlying human cognition. One aspect of EEG neuroscience that continues to attract attention is the study of theta waves (3-8 Hz). Theta waves are often associated with relaxation, meditation, and sleep, but their role in various neurological conditions remains poorly understood. Recent advancements in AI-powered signal processing have made it possible to extract valuable insights from EEG signals, revolutionizing our understanding of neural dynamics.

The Problem:

Real-time analysis of EEG signals is a daunting task due to the vast amount of data generated by even brief periods of recording. Current methods often rely on manual annotation or simplistic signal processing techniques, leading to inaccurate results and limited clinical utility. Furthermore, most existing solutions fail to account for the complex interplay between theta waves and other neural oscillations, hindering our ability to develop effective treatments.

Clisonix EEG Processing - Real Production Code

import numpy as np
from liam_core import BinaryAlgebra

EEG signal processing with vectorized operations

algebra = BinaryAlgebra()
raw_eeg = np.random.randn(64, 256)  # 64 channels, 256 samples

Bandpass filter via FFT (vectorized)

fft = np.fft.rfft(raw_eeg, axis=1)
freqs = np.fft.rfftfreq(256, 1/256)
mask = (freqs >= 0.5) & (freqs <= 45)
filtered = np.fft.irfft(fft * mask, axis=1)

Feature extraction with matrix operations

alpha_power = algebra.frobenius_norm(filtered[:, 8:13])
print(f"Alpha band power: {alpha_power:.2f}")
