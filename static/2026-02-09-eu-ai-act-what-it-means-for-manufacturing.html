The European Union's Artificial Intelligence Act (AI Act) is set to revolutionize the way AI systems are developed, deployed, and regulated in Europe. As of April 2024, manufacturers will be required to adhere to strict guidelines governing the design, development, and deployment of AI-powered systems. In this article, we'll delve into the implications of the EU AI Act for manufacturing and explore how Clisonix's cutting-edge technologies can help businesses navigate these changes.

The Problem: Real Challenges in Regulatory Compliance

Manufacturers face numerous challenges when it comes to regulatory compliance. Ensuring that AI systems meet the requirements set forth by the EU AI Act is a daunting task, especially considering the rapidly evolving landscape of AI research and development. The lack of clarity around what constitutes "high-risk" AI systems has led to uncertainty among manufacturers, causing many to hesitate in adopting cutting-edge technologies.

Moreover, the traditional approach to regulatory compliance relies heavily on manual audits and assessments, which can be time-consuming and resource-intensive. With the increasing complexity of AI systems, this approach becomes increasingly impractical, leading to the need for more efficient and effective solutions.
