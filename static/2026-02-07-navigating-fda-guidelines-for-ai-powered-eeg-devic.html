The Problem: Regulatory Compliance in Medical Settings

FDA regulations for medical devices are notoriously complex, and AI-powered EEG devices are no exception. The FDA categorizes these devices as Class II or III, depending on their level of risk, with Class III being the most stringent. To obtain FDA clearance, manufacturers must demonstrate that their devices meet specific performance standards, including accuracy, safety, and effectiveness.

However, ensuring regulatory compliance for AI-powered EEG devices is no easy feat. Traditional methods of validating device performance may not be sufficient when dealing with complex AI algorithms and machine learning models.
