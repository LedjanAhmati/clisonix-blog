The Problem

In enterprise healthcare settings, analyzing large amounts of clinical data from various sources, such as EEGs (electroencephalograms), can be a daunting task. The sheer volume of data generated by these devices is staggering, making it difficult for clinicians to identify patterns and predict outcomes with any degree of certainty. This can lead to delayed diagnosis, suboptimal treatment plans, and ultimately, poorer patient outcomes.

Furthermore, existing solutions often rely on manual processing and interpretation of data, which is time-consuming, labor-intensive, and prone to human error. The use of traditional machine learning algorithms can also be limited by their inability to handle high-dimensional data and complex relationships between variables.
