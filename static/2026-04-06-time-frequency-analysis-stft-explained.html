The Problem

Signal processing is an intricate dance between time and frequency domains. Traditional methods like Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) are insufficient for real-world physiological signals, as they assume stationarity – a fundamental property that rarely holds true in practice. The human body's natural rhythms and fluctuations defy simplistic models, rendering conventional approaches ineffective.

In addition to the non-stationary nature of signals, other challenges arise from:

1. Data dimensionality: Multimodal data fusion, such as EEG, audio, and biosensor streams, creates an explosion of features that are difficult to analyze. 2. Signal variability: Physiological signals exhibit extreme variability across individuals, making it challenging to develop accurate models.

Technical Deep Dive

To overcome these challenges, we rely on advanced signal processing techniques, particularly STFT. This algorithm decomposes a signal into time-localized frequency components, providing a compact representation of the signal's energy distribution over both time and frequency.

STFT's core idea is to divide the input signal into overlapping windows, apply FFT to each window, and then combine the results using a sliding window technique. This approach allows for accurate analysis of non-stationary signals, enabling us to identify patterns that would be lost with traditional methods.
