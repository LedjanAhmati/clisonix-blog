As we continue to push the boundaries of artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML), it's becoming increasingly clear that our industry has a significant impact on the environment. The production and deployment of AI models require massive amounts of computing power, energy consumption, and data storage – all of which contribute to greenhouse gas emissions and climate change.

The Problem

Sustainable tech is not just a nice-to-have; it's a must-have. According to a report by the Natural Resources Defense Council (NRDC), the digital economy accounts for approximately 1% of global electricity consumption, but this number is expected to grow exponentially with the increasing adoption of AI and ML.

Moreover, the environmental impact of data centers alone is staggering. A study by the International Energy Agency (IEA) found that data centers consume more than 2% of global electricity and contribute around 0.3% of global greenhouse gas emissions.
