The Problem: Real Challenges in Audio Processing

Audio processing has long been a crucial component of healthcare technology, from speech recognition systems to medical imaging devices. However, traditional audio processing techniques often rely on manual annotation and feature extraction methods, which are time-consuming, labor-intensive, and prone to human error. Moreover, the sheer volume of data generated by audio signals in healthcare settings can overwhelm even the most advanced processing systems.

To make matters worse, existing audio processing frameworks often fail to account for the complex interplay between audio, biosensor, and EEG streams. This can lead to inconsistencies and inaccuracies in diagnosis and treatment recommendations. The stakes are high: a single misdiagnosis or delayed treatment can have catastrophic consequences for patients and their families.
