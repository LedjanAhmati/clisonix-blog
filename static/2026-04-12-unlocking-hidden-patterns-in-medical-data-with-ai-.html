The Problem

Current approaches to EEG signal processing rely on traditional methods, which often fall short in capturing the full complexity of brain activity. These limitations are twofold: first, they fail to account for the high-dimensional nature of EEG signals; second, they neglect the inherent non-linearity and spatial relationships within these signals. The result is a fragmented understanding of neurological disorders, with each patient's unique characteristics remaining opaque.

To make matters worse, current AI solutions often rely on shallow feature extraction methods, which neglect the intricate patterns that underlie brain activity. As a consequence, we see suboptimal performance in diagnostic accuracy and a failure to generalize across different patient populations.

Technical Deep Dive
