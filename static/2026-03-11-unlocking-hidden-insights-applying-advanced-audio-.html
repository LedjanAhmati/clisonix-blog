The Problem: Real Challenges in Audio Processing

Audio processing has long been a cornerstone of various industries, including music production, speech recognition, and acoustic signal analysis. However, its application in medical diagnostics remains limited due to several challenges:

1. Signal complexity: Medical audio signals often exhibit complex patterns, making it difficult for traditional signal processing techniques to extract meaningful information. 2. Noise sensitivity: Audio signals are prone to noise corruption, which can lead to inaccurate diagnoses and ineffective treatments. 3. Interoperability issues: Integrating audio analytics with existing medical systems poses significant technical hurdles.
