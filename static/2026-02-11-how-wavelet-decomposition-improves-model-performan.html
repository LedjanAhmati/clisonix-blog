The Problem Real-world AI/ML systems face numerous challenges:

* Scalability: As the volume of data grows, traditional processing methods become bottlenecked. * Noise and artifacts: Raw sensor data often contains noise and artifacts that can compromise model accuracy. * Concept drift: Shifts in underlying patterns can cause models to degrade over time.

To address these issues, researchers have turned to wavelet decomposition ‚Äì a signal processing technique that breaks down signals into their constituent frequency components. By applying wavelet transforms to raw sensor data, AI/ML systems can:

* Improve feature extraction: Wavelets capture subtle patterns and anomalies in the data. * Enhance noise robustness: Wavelet-based denoising techniques can filter out irrelevant noise.
