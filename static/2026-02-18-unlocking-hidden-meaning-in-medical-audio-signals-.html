As healthcare providers continue to grapple with the growing demand for high-quality patient care, the importance of accurate diagnosis and treatment has never been more pressing. The Problem

Audio signals in healthcare encompass a wide range of modalities, including EEG, ECG, and audio recordings of patient symptoms. While traditional signal processing methods may be able to extract some basic features, they often fall short when it comes to uncovering the subtle patterns and relationships hidden within these signals. For instance, changes in heart rate variability or brain activity can indicate underlying conditions such as stress, anxiety, or even cardiovascular disease.

Moreover, the sheer volume of audio data generated by medical devices poses significant challenges for healthcare providers. Manual analysis is time-consuming, prone to human error, and often impossible to scale with increasing patient populations. The need for advanced signal processing techniques that can accurately identify and extract relevant information from these signals has never been more pressing.
