The Problem: Real Challenges in Signal Processing

Signal processing is a complex task that involves extracting meaningful information from raw data. Medical signals, such as EEG and ECG readings, are particularly challenging due to their high-frequency components and noise. Current signal processing techniques often fall short, leading to inaccurate or incomplete interpretations of medical data.

For instance:

* Many algorithms struggle with non-stationarity, which arises when the statistical properties of a signal change over time. * Others fail to account for the inherent complexity of biomedical signals, which can exhibit multiple frequency components and nonlinear relationships. * Moreover, existing techniques often require extensive manual intervention, limiting their scalability and efficiency.

Technical Deep Dive: Architecture, Algorithms, Implementation

To overcome these challenges, Clisonix's technologies employ advanced signal processing techniques that leverage the strengths of both Tide Engine and Signal Fabric. These include:

1. Multi-resolution analysis: This approach enables us to analyze signals at multiple scales, uncovering patterns that might be missed with traditional methods. 2. Sparse signal representations: By applying algorithms like Compressed Sensing (CS), we can efficiently reconstruct signals from their constituent parts, even in the presence of noise and missing data. 3. Deep learning-based architectures: Our Signal Fabric technology uses deep neural networks to automatically learn optimal feature extractions and patterns from complex signals.
