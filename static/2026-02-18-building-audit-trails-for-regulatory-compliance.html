As the healthcare industry continues to adopt innovative technologies like AI and machine learning, regulatory compliance has become an increasingly complex challenge. With the introduction of the EU's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) in 2018 and the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) in the US, healthcare organizations must now ensure that their digital systems are equipped to handle rigorous audit trails.

At Clisonix, we've witnessed firsthand the difficulties our clients face when attempting to build comprehensive audit trails. Our Tide Engine ensures consistent state across distributed healthcare nodes, while our Signal Fabric weaves together EEG, audio, and biosensor streams. But even with these robust technologies in place, regulatory compliance can still be a daunting task.

The Problem

Traditional approaches to building audit trails are often ad hoc, relying on manual logging or incomplete automated systems. This can lead to several issues:

1. Incomplete data: Without comprehensive audit trail capabilities, organizations may struggle to reconstruct past events accurately. 2. Insufficient context: Lacking contextual information, auditors find it challenging to analyze events and identify potential security breaches. 3. Inadequate reporting: The inability to generate detailed reports on system activities can hinder compliance efforts.

To overcome these challenges, healthcare organizations must adopt a more structured approach to building audit trails that captures all relevant data points.
