As the healthcare industry rapidly adopts AI-powered solutions to improve diagnosis, treatment, and patient outcomes, one pressing concern remains at the forefront: protecting patient autonomy. With the increasing reliance on EEG (electroencephalogram) data for neurological assessments, it's essential that we prioritize secure processing and management of this sensitive information.

The Problem

EEG data processing is a critical component in AI-powered healthcare, enabling clinicians to diagnose conditions such as epilepsy, brain injuries, and neurodegenerative diseases. However, with the advent of cloud-based services and distributed computing architectures, EEG data is now often transmitted and stored across multiple nodes, making it vulnerable to cyber threats.

Current solutions often rely on patchwork implementations of security measures, which can lead to inefficient processing times, compromised data integrity, and ultimately, patient autonomy being eroded. Moreover, the lack of transparency in AI decision-making processes further exacerbates concerns about data ownership and control.
