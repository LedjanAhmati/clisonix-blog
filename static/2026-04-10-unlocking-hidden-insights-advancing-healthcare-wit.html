The Problem: Real Challenges in Audio Processing

Audio signal processing is a vital component of healthcare diagnostics, particularly in areas such as speech therapy, hearing loss, and cardiovascular disease. However, the sheer complexity and variability of audio data pose significant challenges:

* Variability: Audio signals are inherently dynamic, influenced by factors like environmental noise, equipment quality, and patient conditions. * Complexity: Signals often consist of multiple frequency components, requiring sophisticated processing techniques to extract meaningful information. * Scalability: As healthcare datasets grow exponentially, traditional audio processing methods struggle to keep pace.
