The Problem: Edge Computing in Healthcare

Edge computing is a distributed computing paradigm that enables processing and analysis of data at or near the source of generation. In healthcare, this means analyzing vast amounts of data from EEG sensors, biosensors, and other wearables to extract meaningful insights. However, current edge computing solutions fall short:

1. Scalability: Many edge computing platforms struggle to scale with growing datasets, leading to performance degradation and decreased accuracy. 2. Latency: Real-time processing is crucial in healthcare; however, most edge computing solutions introduce unacceptable latency due to centralized data processing. 3. Security: Edge devices often lack robust security measures, making them vulnerable to cyber threats and data breaches.
