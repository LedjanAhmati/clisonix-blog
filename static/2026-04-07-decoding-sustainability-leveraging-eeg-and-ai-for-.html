As the world grapples with the challenges of climate change, sustainable technology has become an essential aspect of modern innovation. In the healthcare industry, where medical devices rely heavily on non-renewable energy sources, optimizing energy harvesting is crucial for reducing environmental impact. Clisonix, a pioneer in healthcare AI solutions, has developed cutting-edge technologies that leverage EEG (electroencephalography) and AI to optimize medical device energy harvesting.

The Problem

Medical devices, such as pacemakers, implantable cardioverter-defibrillators (ICDs), and insulin pumps, rely on battery-powered energy sources. These batteries often require frequent replacements, contributing to electronic waste and environmental pollution. Moreover, the increasing demand for medical devices has led to concerns about energy efficiency and sustainability.

Machine learning network — Photo: Unsplash

Conventional approaches to optimizing energy harvesting have focused on hardware-based solutions, such as advanced battery technologies or energy-harvesting materials. However, these methods often come with limitations, including high costs, reduced lifespan, and decreased performance.

Technical Deep Dive

Clisonix's innovative solution harnesses the power of EEG signals to optimize medical device energy harvesting. By leveraging our proprietary Signal Fabric technology, we integrate EEG streams with audio and biosensor data streams in real-time. This allows for the creation of a rich, multi-dimensional representation of physiological patterns, which are then analyzed using AI-driven algorithms.

Our Tide Engine technology ensures consistent state across distributed healthcare nodes, enabling seamless communication between devices and facilitating real-time optimization of energy harvesting. By leveraging these technologies, Clisonix has developed an EEG-based energy-harvesting system that can predict and adapt to changing energy demands in medical devices.
