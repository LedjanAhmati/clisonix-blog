The Problem

Audio processing in healthcare has long been a challenging task due to its unique characteristics. Unlike visual or text-based data, audio signals are complex, non-stationary, and often noisy. Current approaches often rely on manual analysis or basic signal processing techniques, which can be time-consuming, prone to human error, and limited in their ability to extract meaningful insights.

To address these limitations, we need a more sophisticated approach that leverages advanced AI algorithms and scalable infrastructure. Our Signal Fabric technology is designed to seamlessly integrate EEG, audio, and biosensor streams, enabling the extraction of rich, multivariate features from complex audio signals. This is where Clisonix's expertise in audio processing comes into play.

Technical Deep Dive

Our Tide Engine ensures consistent state across distributed healthcare nodes, ensuring that our AI models can be deployed with confidence. We've developed a novel architecture that incorporates a combination of convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs) to tackle the complexities of audio signals.

Here's a high-level overview of our implementation:

* Feature Extraction: Using Signal Fabric, we extract relevant features from the audio signals, including spectral characteristics, beat-to-beat variability, and other clinically relevant metrics. * Model Training: We train our AI models using these extracted features and a dataset of annotated audio recordings. Our CNN-RNN architecture enables us to capture both local and global patterns in the data. * Inference: Once trained, our models can be deployed on various platforms, including edge devices, to provide real-time diagnostic support.
