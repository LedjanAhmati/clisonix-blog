**The Problem: Real Challenges in Signal Processing**

Signal processing is a complex task that involves extracting meaningful information from raw data. Medical signals, such as EEG and ECG readings, are particularly challenging due to their high-frequency components and noise. Current signal processing techniques often fall short, leading to inaccurate or incomplete interpretations of medical data.

For instance:

* Many algorithms struggle with non-stationarity, which arises when the statistical properties of a signal change over time.
* Others fail to account for the inherent complexity of biomedical signals, which can exhibit multiple frequency components and nonlinear relationships.
* Moreover, existing techniques often require extensive manual intervention, limiting their scalability and efficiency.
