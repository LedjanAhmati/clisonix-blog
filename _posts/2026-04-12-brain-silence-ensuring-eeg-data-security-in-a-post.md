In the post-GDPR world, healthcare apps face unprecedented scrutiny over data privacy. With the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) in effect since 2018, organizations handling sensitive medical information are held accountable for protecting it. Brain-Silence is a pressing concern in this context, as EEG data – collected using electroencephalography technology – is particularly vulnerable to unauthorized access and misuse.

**The Problem**

EEG data contains highly personal and sensitive information about an individual's brain activity. Healthcare apps relying on this data must ensure its confidentiality, integrity, and availability to prevent breaches. The consequences of a data leak can be severe, including compromised patient trust, reputational damage, and costly fines under GDPR.

However, ensuring EEG data security is more complex than just implementing encryption or firewalls. Data streams from EEG devices are typically large, unstructured, and noisy, making them challenging to handle and analyze. Moreover, the sheer volume of data generated by these devices creates a daunting task for healthcare apps to process and store securely.
