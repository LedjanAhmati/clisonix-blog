**The Problem: Data Privacy in Healthcare**

In today's digital age, the vast majority of healthcare data is generated through brain signal technologies like EEG, fMRI, or EEG-fMRI. These signals are often paired with audio, biosensor streams, and other physiological metrics to create a rich tapestry of medical insights. However, this data is sensitive and potentially identifiable, raising concerns about patient confidentiality and informed consent.

In reality, many existing healthcare AI solutions struggle to safeguard these digital assets while still providing actionable intelligence. They often rely on shallow anonymization techniques or manual review processes that are both time-consuming and error-prone. This can lead to incomplete or inaccurate data analysis, ultimately compromising the quality of care delivered to patients.
