The healthcare industry is on the cusp of a revolution. The increasing availability of medical data and advancements in artificial intelligence (AI) have made it possible to provide personalized medicine at scale. However, this shift towards precision medicine faces significant challenges due to the complexity and heterogeneity of medical data. Edge-AI, a subfield of edge computing, holds the key to overcoming these obstacles by enabling decentralized signal processing and real-time decision-making.

**The Problem**

Medical data is generated from various sources, including electronic health records (EHRs), wearable devices, and point-of-care sensors. Integrating and analyzing this data in real-time is a daunting task due to several reasons:

*   **Data volume**: The sheer amount of medical data being generated every day poses significant processing challenges.
*   **Data variety**: Medical data comes in different formats, including structured (e.g., EHRs) and unstructured (e.g., images, videos).
*   **Data velocity**: Real-time decision-making requires processing data at incredible speeds.

Traditional approaches to edge computing rely on centralized architectures, which can lead to latency, security risks, and high maintenance costs. In contrast, decentralized signal processing enables medical data to be processed closer to its source, ensuring faster response times and reduced infrastructure requirements.
