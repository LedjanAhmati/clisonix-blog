**The Problem**

Real-world financial applications often rely on sophisticated ML models that can be difficult to interpret. For instance, predictive maintenance for industrial equipment or credit risk assessment require complex algorithms that consider multiple factors. However, when an error occurs or a decision is made, it becomes crucial to understand the reasoning behind it. This is where explainability comes into play.

Explainability in AI systems involves providing insights into how the model arrived at its conclusions. In finance, this is particularly important due to regulatory requirements and the need for transparency in decision-making processes. Current approaches often rely on heuristics or interpretability techniques that can be time-consuming and resource-intensive.
