**The Problem: Real Challenges in Signal Processing**

Signal processing is a fundamental aspect of healthcare AI, yet it often poses significant challenges due to the complex nature of biomedical signals. In the context of EEG, these difficulties arise from:

1. **High-dimensional data**: EEG signals produce high-resolution datasets with thousands of dimensions, making them notoriously challenging to process and analyze.
2. **Noise and artifacts**: EEG signals are prone to noise and artifacts, which can significantly impact signal quality and accuracy.
3. **Variable sampling rates**: The sampling rate of EEG recordings varies greatly between devices and institutions, complicating data integration and analysis.

To overcome these challenges, researchers have turned to advanced signal processing techniques. However, many existing approaches fall short due to their lack of scalability, adaptability, or interpretability.
