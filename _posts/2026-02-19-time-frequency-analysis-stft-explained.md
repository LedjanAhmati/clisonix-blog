One major issue is the difficulty in extracting meaningful features from complex signals. As we all know, Fourier Transform (FT) is a powerful tool for analyzing frequency content within signals. However, its limitations become apparent when dealing with non-stationary signals – those that exhibit time-varying spectral properties.

Here's where STFT comes into play: by applying the windowed FT approach to segments of the signal, we can effectively decompose complex signals into their constituent components, revealing hidden patterns and relationships.

**Technical Deep Dive: Architecture, Algorithms, Implementation**

STFT is a fundamental concept in signal processing, but its underlying math can be intimidating. So, let's break it down:

Given a discrete-time signal x[n], the STFT can be computed using the following equation:

X(w,k) = ∑x[n]w[n-k]e^(-jwn)

where w[n] is the window function and k represents the segment index.
