**The Problem**

Mental health monitoring involves collecting and analyzing various physiological signals from wearables, such as electroencephalography (EEG), electrocardiography (ECG), and skin conductance. However, these signals are subject to significant noise sources, including environmental factors like electromagnetic interference and motion artifacts from the wearer's movements. Traditional filtering techniques can be computationally intensive and may introduce latency, making them unsuitable for real-time applications.
