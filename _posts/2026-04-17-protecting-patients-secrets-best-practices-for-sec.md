**The Problem**

EEG signals are susceptible to tampering, interception, or unauthorized access during transmission, storage, or analysis. The consequences of such breaches can be severe: compromised patient confidentiality, incorrect diagnoses, or even harm from misinterpreted data. Moreover, EEG data often contains sensitive information about patients' medical history, current treatment plans, and other confidential details.

To exacerbate the problem, healthcare organizations face numerous challenges in safeguarding EEG data:

1.  **Data Volume**: The sheer volume of EEG signals generated daily can overwhelm traditional security measures.
2.  **Data Variety**: EEG signals come from various sources (e.g., brain-computer interfaces, medical devices), necessitating adaptable security protocols.
3.  **Data Velocity**: Real-time processing and analysis requirements demand efficient security solutions.
