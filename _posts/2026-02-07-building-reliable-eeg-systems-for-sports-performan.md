Another significant challenge lies in developing systems that can adapt to changing environments. In sports settings, athletes often move around or engage in high-intensity activities, which can disrupt EEG signals. Furthermore, EEG-based systems must be able to integrate with other data streams – such as audio and biosensors – to provide a comprehensive understanding of an athlete's performance.
