The Problem: Real Challenges in Data Privacy

As EEG (electroencephalogram) data becomes increasingly valuable for clinical decision-making and research, concerns about its handling, storage, and processing have grown exponentially. The sheer volume and complexity of EEG signals pose significant challenges to maintaining data integrity and ensuring that sensitive information remains confidential.

With the growing adoption of AI-powered healthcare solutions, there's an inherent risk of compromising patient trust if data is not handled with utmost care. Mismanagement or exploitation of EEG data can lead to severe consequences, including:

1. **Data breaches**: Unsecured EEG data storage facilities can be vulnerable to cyberattacks, exposing sensitive information and putting patients at risk.
2. **Bias in algorithms**: AI models trained on biased datasets can perpetuate existing healthcare disparities, leading to suboptimal treatment decisions and compromised patient outcomes.
3. **Over-reliance on technology**: Overemphasis on AI-driven diagnosis and treatment may erode the trust between patients and healthcare providers.
