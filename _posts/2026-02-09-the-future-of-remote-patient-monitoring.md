Remote patient monitoring has become increasingly essential in modern healthcare. With the rise of digital health technologies and telemedicine, patients can now receive timely care and attention from anywhere in the world. However, this shift also brings its own set of challenges. Healthcare systems must navigate complex data management, ensure seamless communication between clinicians, and protect sensitive medical information.

**The Problem**

Traditional remote patient monitoring relies on outdated infrastructure and manual workflows. Clinicians often struggle to access up-to-date patient data, leading to delayed diagnoses and treatments. Moreover, the sheer volume of patient data generated by IoT devices, wearables, and electronic health records (EHRs) creates a massive burden for healthcare providers. The lack of standardized protocols and interoperability between systems further exacerbates these challenges.

Inadequate security measures also pose significant risks. With sensitive medical information being transmitted remotely, the threat of cyber attacks looms large. Unauthorized access to patient data can lead to identity theft, data breaches, and compromised care. In a survey conducted by Clisonix, 75% of healthcare professionals reported concerns about the security of remote patient monitoring systems.
