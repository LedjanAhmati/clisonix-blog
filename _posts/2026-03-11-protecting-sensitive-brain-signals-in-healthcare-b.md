**The Problem**

EEG signals are a crucial component of various medical applications, including neurology, psychology, and neuroscience research. However, they also present several challenges when it comes to data privacy:

1.  **Signal complexity**: EEG signals exhibit high temporal resolution, with frequencies ranging from delta (0.5-4 Hz) to gamma bands (>40 Hz). This complexity makes them prone to overfitting and requires sophisticated signal processing techniques.
2.  **Sensor variability**: EEG recordings are often obtained using multiple sensors, which can introduce inconsistencies in data quality and accuracy.
3.  **Subjective interpretations**: Human interpretation of EEG signals is inherently subjective, leading to potential misdiagnoses or incorrect conclusions.

To mitigate these risks, healthcare providers must implement robust data protection measures that ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability (CIA) of EEG signals.
