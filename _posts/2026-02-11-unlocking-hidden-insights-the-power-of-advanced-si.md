**The Problem: Real Challenges in Signal Processing**

Signal processing is a crucial aspect of medical device development, as it enables the extraction of meaningful information from raw sensor data. However, traditional approaches to signal processing often fall short, leading to inaccurate diagnoses, suboptimal treatments, and wasted resources. The challenges are multifaceted:

1. **Noise and interference**: Sensor noise, electrical interference, and other forms of corruption can significantly degrade signal quality.
2. **Complexity and dimensionality**: Medical signals often exhibit intricate patterns and relationships, making it difficult to tease out actionable insights.
3. **Time-series analysis**: Signals from medical devices typically involve time-series data, which requires specialized techniques for processing and visualization.
