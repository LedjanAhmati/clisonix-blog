**The Problem**

EEG data is highly sensitive and personal, containing information about an individual's cognitive state, emotional responses, and even their most intimate thoughts. As such, it's essential that any system processing or storing EEG data does so in a way that prioritizes patient confidentiality and data protection. Unfortunately, the current landscape of EEG ecosystem security falls short.

One major challenge lies in the decentralized nature of EEG data collection. With the rise of wearable devices and mobile apps, EEG signals are being generated from diverse sources, often in uncontrolled environments. This makes it difficult to ensure that sensitive data is transmitted securely and stored safely. Moreover, EEG signal processing itself can introduce vulnerabilities, as algorithms may inadvertently reveal confidential information or compromise patient anonymity.
