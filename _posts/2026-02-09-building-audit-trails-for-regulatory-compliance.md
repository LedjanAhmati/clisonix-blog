**The Problem**

Unfortunately, building effective audit trails is a significant challenge for many healthcare organizations. The sheer volume of data generated by modern healthcare systems makes it difficult to track changes and identify potential security risks. Moreover, the complexity of these systems often leads to fragmented and incomplete audit logs, making it even harder to detect anomalies.

To make matters worse, healthcare organizations must comply with a multitude of regulations, each with its own set of requirements for audit trails. For instance, HIPAA requires that covered entities maintain an accurate and complete record of all electronic protected health information (ePHI) accessed or modified. Similarly, GDPR mandates that personal data be handled in accordance with the principles of transparency, security, and accountability.
