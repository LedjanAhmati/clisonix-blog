**The Problem: Real Challenges in Data Privacy**

Patient data is considered sensitive personal information (SPI), subject to various regulatory requirements such as HIPAA, GDPR, and others. Healthcare organizations must ensure that patient data is safeguarded against unauthorized access, theft, or misuse. The consequences of a data breach can be severe, including financial penalties, reputational damage, and compromised patient trust.

Current challenges in data privacy include:

1. **Data fragmentation**: EEG and audio recordings are often generated by multiple devices, creating disparate datasets that require careful integration.
2. **Synchronization issues**: Ensuring the consistency of brain signals and audio streams across distributed nodes is a complex problem.
3. **Security vulnerabilities**: AI models and algorithms can introduce new security risks if not properly designed and implemented.
