**FAQ**

**Q: What are the key considerations for BCI device classification and labeling?**
A: The FDA has established specific guidelines for classifying BCI devices as Class I, II, or III. Classification is based on factors such as device functionality, patient risk, and intended use. Labeling requirements include clear instructions for use, warnings, and cautions.

**Q: How can we ensure data integrity in distributed healthcare systems?**
A: Clisonix's Tide Engine utilizes a combination of consensus algorithms and data validation techniques to maintain consistent state across nodes. This ensures accurate and reliable data exchange between devices.

**Q: What are the implications of FDA guidelines for device software updates and maintenance?**
A: The FDA requires that BCI device software updates and maintenance procedures be clearly documented, validated, and regularly audited. This includes implementing change control processes to ensure compliance with regulatory requirements.

**Q: Can we use off-the-shelf components in our BCI devices without affecting compliance?**
A: While using off-the-shelf components can simplify development, it is essential to ensure that these components meet FDA standards for safety and efficacy. Clisonix recommends carefully evaluating the suitability of third-party components before integrating them into your products.

**Q: How do we handle cybersecurity risks in BCI devices?**
A: The FDA emphasizes the importance of implementing robust cybersecurity measures to protect against unauthorized access, data breaches, or other security threats. This includes conducting regular vulnerability assessments and penetration testing.
