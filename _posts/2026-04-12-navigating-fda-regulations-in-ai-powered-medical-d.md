**The Problem**

Developing AI-powered medical devices that comply with FDA regulations can be daunting. The 510(k) clearance process requires manufacturers to demonstrate substantial equivalence to an existing device or predicate device. Additionally, the FDA has established guidelines for the development and validation of AI algorithms used in medical devices. Deviating from these guidelines can lead to costly delays and even product recalls.

To make matters more complicated, regulatory requirements are not one-size-fits-all. Different types of devices have varying levels of complexity, and different regulatory bodies may have distinct standards for approval. For instance, the FDA's Center for Devices and Radiological Health (CDRH) has separate guidelines for software as a medical device (SaMD), while the European Union's Medical Device Regulation (MDR) has its own set of requirements.
