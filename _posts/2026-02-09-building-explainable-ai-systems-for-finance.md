The recent collapse of FTX and its ripple effects on the global financial landscape have sparked concerns about the trustworthiness of artificial intelligence (AI) models in finance. As regulators and investors alike scrutinize the use of AI in high-stakes decision-making, it's imperative that we address the lack of transparency and accountability in these systems.

**The Problem**

Explainable AI (XAI) is a critical component of trustworthy AI systems. In finance, XAI enables stakeholders to understand how AI-driven models make decisions, ensuring that these models align with regulatory requirements and investor expectations. However, building explainable AI systems for finance poses unique challenges:

1. **Complexity**: Financial data is inherently complex, comprising multiple variables, non-linear relationships, and high-frequency transactions.
2. **Interpretability**: XAI methods must provide clear explanations of model decisions, without sacrificing accuracy or performance.
3. **Regulatory compliance**: Finance AI systems must meet stringent regulatory requirements, such as those set by the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority (FINRA) and the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC).
