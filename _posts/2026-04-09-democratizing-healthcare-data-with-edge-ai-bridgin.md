**The Problem: Edge Computing Challenges**

Edge computing has gained significant traction in recent years, but its application in healthcare is hindered by several obstacles. These include:

*   **Data silos**: Isolated repositories of patient data stored on individual devices or within clinical systems, leading to difficulties in sharing and aggregating information.
*   **Device heterogeneity**: The multitude of medical devices with varying communication protocols and data formats hinders seamless integration and real-time processing.
*   **Latency and bandwidth constraints**: Healthcare professionals require timely access to data for informed decision-making. However, traditional cloud-centric architectures often introduce unacceptable latency and bandwidth limitations.

To overcome these challenges, we need a paradigm shift in how edge computing is applied in healthcare – one that enables the democratization of data and empowers medical professionals with real-time insights.
