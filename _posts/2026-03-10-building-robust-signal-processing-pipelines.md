**The Problem**

Signal processing is a complex task that involves various steps, including filtering, transformation, feature extraction, and analysis. However, healthcare-specific signal processing pipelines face additional challenges:

1. **Data heterogeneity**: Signals from different sources (e.g., EEG, audio, biosensors) have distinct characteristics, making it difficult to develop a unified processing framework.
2. **Temporal and spatial variability**: Health signals can exhibit complex temporal patterns and spatial relationships, requiring specialized algorithms to capture these nuances.
3. **Scalability and fault tolerance**: As the number of connected devices grows, signal processing pipelines must ensure consistent performance across distributed nodes and adapt to hardware failures or connectivity issues.

**Technical Deep Dive**
