**The Problem**

Data privacy is a pressing concern in healthcare due to the sensitivity and volume of patient data handled by care providers. The consequences of a data breach can be severe, including compromised patient safety, reputational damage, and financial losses. Furthermore, the complexity of modern healthcare systems, with their interconnected devices and services, creates numerous entry points for potential threats.

The problem is multifaceted:

1. **Data proliferation**: The increasing amount of data generated in healthcare, from medical imaging to genomic sequencing, poses significant storage and processing challenges.
2. **Interoperability**: The lack of standardized data formats and communication protocols makes it difficult for different systems and devices to exchange information securely.
3. **Cybersecurity threats**: Healthcare is a prime target for cyber attacks due to the high value of patient data on the dark web.
