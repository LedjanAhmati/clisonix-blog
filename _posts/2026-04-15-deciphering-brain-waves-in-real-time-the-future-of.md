**The Problem**

EEG signal processing poses several challenges that hinder its widespread adoption. Firstly, high-dimensional EEG signals are prone to noise and artifacts, making it difficult to extract meaningful information from raw data. Secondly, the sheer volume of data generated by long-term monitoring or multiple patients necessitates robust data management and analysis frameworks.

To address these challenges, healthcare providers require sophisticated signal processing techniques that can handle large datasets efficiently while maintaining accuracy. Existing solutions often rely on manual annotation or suboptimal algorithms, leading to delayed diagnosis and reduced treatment efficacy.
