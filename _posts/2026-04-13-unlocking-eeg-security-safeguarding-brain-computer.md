**The Problem: Real Challenges in Data Privacy**

EEG signals are particularly vulnerable to data breaches due to their high dimensionality and complexity. A single EEG recording can generate tens of gigabytes of data, making it challenging to safeguard against unauthorized access. Moreover, the increasing adoption of cloud-based storage solutions has created new security risks, as sensitive data is transmitted across networks and stored in remote servers.

Furthermore, traditional encryption methods often struggle to protect EEG data due to its unique characteristics. EEG signals are not just numerical values; they contain subtle patterns and oscillations that can be easily corrupted or manipulated during transmission. This makes it difficult for conventional encryption algorithms to provide adequate protection against unauthorized access.
