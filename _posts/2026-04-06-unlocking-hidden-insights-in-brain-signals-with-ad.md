**The Problem**

EEG signal processing is a complex task due to several factors:

1. **Signal complexity**: EEG signals are inherently noisy, non-stationary, and high-dimensional, making them challenging to analyze.
2. **Time-frequency analysis**: Capturing the intricate temporal and spectral patterns within EEG signals requires sophisticated time-frequency analysis techniques.
3. **Data dimensionality**: EEG signals often involve a large number of channels (up to 256), leading to vast amounts of data that need to be processed efficiently.

To overcome these challenges, researchers rely on advanced signal processing techniques, including wavelet analysis, Independent Component Analysis (ICA), and Machine Learning (ML). However, these methods can be computationally intensive, making them difficult to implement in real-world scenarios.
