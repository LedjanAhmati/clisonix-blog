**The Problem: Real Challenges in Regulatory Compliance**

Regulatory compliance is often seen as an afterthought or a mere checkbox exercise, but it's essential to address these concerns proactively. The Food and Drug Administration (FDA) regulates medical devices, including BCIs, under the 510(k) program for moderate-risk devices and the Premarket Approval (PMA) process for high-risk devices. Compliance with FDA regulations requires significant expertise in both regulatory affairs and software development.

Current BCI software development efforts often struggle to meet these stringent requirements due to:

1. **Lack of clear guidelines**: The FDA provides guidance, but there is still a need for specific rules and standards governing the development of AI-powered BCIs.
2. **Data management and analysis**: Accurate data collection, analysis, and interpretation are critical components of BCI software, which can be challenging to navigate within regulatory frameworks.
3. **Risk-based approach**: Determining the risk level associated with a particular BCI device or system is a complex process that requires thorough understanding of both the technology and the regulations.
