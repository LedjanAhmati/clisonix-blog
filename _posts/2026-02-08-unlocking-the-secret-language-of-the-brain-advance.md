**The Problem**

Signal processing in EEG is a notoriously difficult task due to several reasons:

1. **Noise and artifacts**: EEG signals are susceptible to various forms of noise, such as muscle activity, eye movement, and electrical interference from external sources.
2. **Variability across individuals**: Each person's brain activity exhibits unique patterns, making it challenging to establish a common framework for analysis.
3. **High-dimensional data**: EEG signals consist of multiple channels (typically 32 or more), resulting in an enormous amount of data that requires efficient processing techniques.
