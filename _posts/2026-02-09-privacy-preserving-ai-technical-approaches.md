**The Problem**
Data privacy is more than just a regulatory requirement - it's a fundamental principle of trust in healthcare. Patients entrust their providers with their most intimate and personal information, expecting that it will be kept confidential and protected from unauthorized access or misuse. However, as we increasingly rely on AI to analyze and learn from this data, the risk of breaches and unintended disclosure grows.

Consider the following scenarios:

* A patient's medical records are leaked due to a cybersecurity vulnerability in a healthcare provider's system.
* An AI model trained on sensitive health data is used for malicious purposes, such as predicting patient vulnerabilities or exploiting treatment preferences.
* A healthcare organization's data sharing agreements with third-party vendors are opaque, making it difficult for patients to understand how their information will be used.

These scenarios highlight the tension between the benefits of AI-driven insights and the need for robust data protection.
