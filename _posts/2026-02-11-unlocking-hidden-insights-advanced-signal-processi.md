**The Problem: Real Challenges in Signal Processing**

Signal processing is a critical component of healthcare AI, responsible for extracting valuable patterns and features from physiological signals such as EEG, ECG, and blood pressure readings. However, the sheer volume and complexity of modern biomedical data pose significant challenges to traditional signal processing methods:

1. **Data noise**: Biomedical signals are inherently noisy, making it difficult to distinguish between true patterns and random fluctuations.
2. **Dimensionality**: Signals often have multiple dimensions (e.g., frequency, time), requiring computationally intensive processing.
3. **Inter- and intra-subject variability**: Individual differences in physiology can lead to inconsistent results across patients.
