As healthcare continues its digital transformation, advanced brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) are revolutionizing patient care. These innovative technologies enable clinicians to decode neural signals, improving diagnosis accuracy, treatment effectiveness, and patient outcomes. However, the increased reliance on AI-driven BCIs also raises significant concerns about patient data privacy.

**The Problem**

Patient data is a sensitive asset that requires robust protection against unauthorized access, breaches, and misuse. Advanced BCIs, such as those utilizing electroencephalography (EEG) or functional near-infrared spectroscopy (fNIRS), generate vast amounts of personal and identifiable information. In the wrong hands, this data can be exploited for malicious purposes, including identity theft, targeted advertising, or even cyber-physical attacks.

Moreover, healthcare organizations must adhere to rigorous regulations, such as HIPAA in the United States, which mandate strict controls over patient data handling and storage. Failure to comply with these regulations can result in severe financial penalties, reputational damage, and loss of public trust.
