**The Problem: Challenges in Edge Computing**

Edge computing is a promising approach to address the scalability and latency issues associated with cloud-based processing. By moving computation closer to where data is generated, edge computing enables real-time signal processing, reduced bandwidth requirements, and improved security. However, implementing edge computing solutions for EEG signal analysis poses unique challenges:

1. **Data Volume and Velocity**: EEG sensors generate massive amounts of high-frequency data, making real-time processing a significant challenge.
2. **Real-Time Signal Processing**: Traditional signal processing algorithms often fail to meet the stringent latency requirements of real-time applications.
3. **Scalability and Flexibility**: Edge computing solutions must be designed to accommodate diverse sensor types, EEG protocols, and healthcare workflows.
