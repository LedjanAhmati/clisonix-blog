**The Problem: Real Challenges in Signal Processing**

Signal processing is inherently challenging due to the high-dimensional nature of EEG data. With thousands of electrodes capturing millisecond-scale oscillations, the sheer volume and complexity of raw data can overwhelm even the most advanced systems. Moreover, subtle variations in signal patterns often correspond to distinct cognitive states or neurological conditions, making it essential to develop algorithms capable of identifying these nuances.

Traditional methods, such as wavelet analysis or independent component analysis (ICA), have struggled to maintain scalability while preserving sensitivity. These limitations stem from their inability to effectively manage the massive amounts of data generated by modern EEG systems. As a result, researchers and clinicians continue to face significant hurdles in extracting meaningful patterns and correlations within these signals.
