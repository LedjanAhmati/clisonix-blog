**The Problem: Real Challenges in Data Privacy**

EEG (electroencephalography) data is a unique challenge when it comes to data protection. The sheer volume and complexity of EEG signals pose significant obstacles for secure transmission, storage, and analysis. Moreover, EEG data often requires advanced processing techniques, such as signal decomposition and feature extraction, which introduce additional security concerns.

Traditional approaches to data protection focus on perimeter-based security measures, such as firewalls and encryption. However, these methods are insufficient in the face of modern threats, including insider attacks, lateral movement, and sophisticated malware. Moreover, EEG data is not only sensitive but also highly contextual, requiring a deep understanding of the underlying neural activity.
