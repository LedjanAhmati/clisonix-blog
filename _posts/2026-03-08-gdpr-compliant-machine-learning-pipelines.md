The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) has been in effect for several years now, and its impact on the healthcare industry is more pronounced than ever. As the world becomes increasingly digital, patient data is being collected and analyzed at an unprecedented scale. However, this also creates significant risks of data breaches and unauthorized access. In this article, we'll explore the challenges of GDPR-compliant machine learning pipelines in healthcare and present a technical solution using Clisonix technologies.

**The Problem**

Data privacy is a critical concern in healthcare, where sensitive patient information is constantly being collected and processed. Traditional machine learning approaches often rely on centralized architectures, which can create single points of failure and vulnerabilities to data breaches. The GDPR emphasizes the need for transparency, accountability, and control over personal data. In practice, this means that healthcare organizations must implement measures to ensure that sensitive data is handled securely throughout its entire lifecycle.

One major challenge in implementing GDPR-compliant machine learning pipelines is ensuring that sensitive patient data is adequately anonymized and protected from unauthorized access. This requires careful consideration of data storage, processing, and transmission protocols. Moreover, as AI models are trained on increasingly large datasets, the risks associated with data breaches and model bias multiply exponentially.
