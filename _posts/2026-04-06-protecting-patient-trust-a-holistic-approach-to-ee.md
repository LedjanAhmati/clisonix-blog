**The Problem: Real Challenges in Data Privacy**

EEG data is particularly vulnerable to cyber threats due to its sensitive nature and the complexity of processing techniques involved. In 2022 alone, over 50% of healthcare organizations reported experiencing a data breach, with EEG data being one of the most prized targets (HIPAA Journal). The consequences can be devastating: compromised patient confidentiality, financial losses, and reputational damage.

Moreover, EEG data is often stored in distributed environments, where it must be accessed and processed by multiple stakeholders. This decentralization introduces additional security risks, as data may reside on unsecured devices or in the cloud. Compliance with regulations such as HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act) becomes even more challenging.
