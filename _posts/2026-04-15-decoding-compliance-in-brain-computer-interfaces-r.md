**The Problem: Real Challenges in Regulatory Compliance**

Regulatory bodies such as the FDA, EU's Medical Device Regulation (MDR), and HIPAA require strict adherence to guidelines for medical device development and deployment. However, traditional compliance frameworks often struggle to keep pace with the rapid evolution of AI and ML technologies. BCI systems, which integrate electrical signals from the brain with AI algorithms, pose unique regulatory challenges due to their complex interplay between hardware, software, and human factors.

For instance, the FDA's 510(k) clearance process for medical devices requires demonstration of substantial equivalence to existing products, but AI-powered BCIs often involve novel technologies and lack clear precedents. Similarly, EU's MDR places emphasis on risk management and clinical evaluation, which can be daunting for companies navigating the intricate web of regulatory requirements.
