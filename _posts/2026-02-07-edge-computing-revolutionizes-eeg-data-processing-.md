**The Problem: Real Challenges in Edge Computing**

Edge computing is a distributed computing paradigm that enables processing to occur at or near the point of data generation. This approach offers several advantages over traditional cloud-based models, including reduced latency, increased efficiency, and improved scalability. However, edge computing also introduces unique challenges when applied to EEG data processing.

One major hurdle lies in managing the high-bandwidth requirements of biomedical signals. With EEG devices generating thousands of samples per second, it's essential to develop efficient compression algorithms that minimize data loss while maintaining signal integrity. Furthermore, ensuring consistent state across distributed healthcare nodes is crucial for real-time analysis and decision-making.
