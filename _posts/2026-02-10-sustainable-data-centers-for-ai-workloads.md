**The Problem: Real Challenges in Sustainable Tech**

Data centers' sustainability challenges are multifaceted:

1. **Energy consumption**: Data centers account for approximately 1% of global electricity usage, which translates to around 200 billion kilowatt-hours annually.
2. **Waste generation**: The e-waste generated by data center hardware disposal is staggering, with estimates suggesting up to 3 million tons of electronic waste are produced globally each year.
3. **Scalability and flexibility**: As AI workloads grow in complexity and scale, data centers must adapt quickly to meet these demands while minimizing their ecological footprint.

Our data center architecture is designed with sustainability in mind:

1. **Containerization**: Utilize lightweight containers (e.g., Docker) for efficient resource allocation and scalability.
2. **Server virtualization**: Leverage hypervisors like KVM or Xen for improved hardware utilization and reduced power consumption.
3. **Energy-efficient cooling systems**: Implement air-side and water-side economizers to minimize energy used for cooling.
