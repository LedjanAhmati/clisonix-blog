**The Problem: Challenges in Edge Computing**

Edge computing is a distributed computing paradigm that enables real-time processing and analysis of data closer to its source. In healthcare, this translates to on-device processing of medical signals, images, and genomic data. However, existing edge computing solutions face several challenges:

1. **Power consumption**: Edge devices, such as medical sensors or wearables, often operate with limited power budgets.
2. **Latency**: Real-time processing and analysis require low latency, which is difficult to achieve with traditional edge computing architectures.
3. **Data transmission**: The sheer volume of biomedical data requires efficient transmission protocols to prevent network congestion.
