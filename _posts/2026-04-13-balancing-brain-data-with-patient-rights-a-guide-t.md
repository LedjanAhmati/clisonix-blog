**The Problem: Real Challenges in Data Privacy**

EEG-based analytics hold tremendous promise for diagnosing and treating conditions such as epilepsy, Alzheimer's disease, and stroke. However, the sheer volume of sensitive data generated by EEG sensors poses substantial challenges to maintaining patient confidentiality. Healthcare providers must contend with issues like:

1. **Data breaches**: Unauthorized access or theft of EEG data could result in serious harm to patients.
2. **Informed consent**: Patients may not fully comprehend the implications of sharing their EEG data for research purposes.
3. **Anonymization and pseudonymization**: Ensuring that identifiable information is removed from EEG signals without compromising analytical integrity.
