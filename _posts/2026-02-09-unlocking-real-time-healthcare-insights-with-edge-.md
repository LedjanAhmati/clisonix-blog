**The Problem: Edge Computing Challenges**

Edge computing is not without its challenges. One major hurdle lies in ensuring consistent state across distributed healthcare nodes. This requires careful management of data consistency, synchronization, and conflict resolution. Furthermore, edge devices often operate under stringent latency constraints, necessitating the development of efficient algorithms that can provide real-time insights while minimizing processing time.

Another challenge arises from the sheer volume and diversity of data being processed at the edge. EEG signals, in particular, pose a significant challenge due to their high-frequency nature and susceptibility to noise and interference.
