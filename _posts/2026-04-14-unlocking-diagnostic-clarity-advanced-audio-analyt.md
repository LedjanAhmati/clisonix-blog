**The Problem**

Audio processing in healthcare has long been plagued by limitations in signal fidelity and robustness. The sheer volume of audio data generated from devices such as EEG machines, medical implants, and even smartphone apps poses significant challenges for accurate analysis. These challenges include:

1. **Signal degradation**: Audio signals are prone to noise, interference, and other distortions that can significantly compromise diagnostic accuracy.
2. **Variability in sampling rates**: Different audio sources have varying sampling rates, making it difficult to standardize processing pipelines.
3. **Inadequate data fusion**: Integrating audio with other vital signs or medical imaging data often leads to inconsistent results due to differing data formats and resolutions.
