**The Problem**

Audio processing is a complex field, plagued by issues such as noise cancellation, echo removal, and speaker identification. Even with the most advanced techniques, it's challenging to extract meaningful information from speech patterns alone. Traditional machine learning models often rely on supervised learning, requiring large datasets labeled by experts – a time-consuming and expensive process.

Moreover, the nuances of human emotion are difficult to quantify. Vocal cues such as pitch, tone, and rhythm can indicate emotional states, but these characteristics are highly context-dependent and sensitive to individual variations. For instance, a person with depression may exhibit a characteristic "flat affect" in their speech, making it hard for AI systems to distinguish from other conditions or normal speech patterns.
