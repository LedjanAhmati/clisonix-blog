As healthcare providers strive to improve patient care and reduce costs, the integration of artificial intelligence (AI) into clinical settings is gaining momentum. However, several technical challenges must be addressed before AI-powered speech recognition can reach its full potential in healthcare.

**The Problem**
Real-world clinical environments pose significant obstacles for speech recognition systems. These include:

1. **Background noise**: Hospitals are inherently noisy places, with beeping equipment, chatter, and other distractions competing for the system's attention.
2. **Diverse speaker populations**: Clinicians from various linguistic and cultural backgrounds may have distinct accents, dialects, or speaking styles that can confuse speech recognition algorithms.
3. **Variable audio quality**: Microphone settings, recording formats, and transmission protocols can introduce inconsistencies in audio data, making it harder for systems to accurately transcribe speech.
