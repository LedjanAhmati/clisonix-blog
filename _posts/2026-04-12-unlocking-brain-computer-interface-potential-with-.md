However, current approaches face several challenges that hinder their adoption in real-world applications.

**The Problem: Real Challenges in AI-ML Systems**

Current BCI systems rely on traditional signal processing techniques, such as Fourier transforms and wavelet analysis, which are computationally intensive and often fail to capture the complexity of neural activity. Moreover, these methods typically require significant expertise in signal processing and neuroscience, making it difficult for researchers and clinicians to integrate BCIs into their workflows.

Furthermore, EEG signals are inherently noisy and non-stationary, requiring advanced algorithms that can handle temporal variability and spatial correlations. Traditional machine learning approaches often struggle with the high dimensionality of EEG data, leading to overfitting and poor generalization performance.
