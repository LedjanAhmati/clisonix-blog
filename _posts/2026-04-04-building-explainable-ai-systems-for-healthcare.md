**The Problem: Real Challenges in AI-ML Systems**

While AI-ML systems have demonstrated remarkable capabilities in identifying patterns within complex medical data, they often fail to provide meaningful insights into their decision-making processes. This phenomenon is commonly referred to as the "black box problem." Without clear explanations for how these systems arrive at their conclusions, healthcare providers and patients are left without a fundamental understanding of the underlying logic.

Furthermore, the opaque nature of AI-ML systems raises critical concerns about bias, accuracy, and accountability. Medical professionals must be able to trust that the AI system is functioning correctly and providing unbiased results. The lack of explainability also hinders the ability to identify errors or areas for improvement within these systems.
