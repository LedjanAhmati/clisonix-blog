In recent years, brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) have gained significant attention in the healthcare technology community. The potential for BCIs to restore mobility, speech, and cognitive function in individuals with neurological disorders has sparked intense research efforts. However, one major challenge remains: enhancing EEG signal quality to accurately interpret brain activity.

**The Problem**

Current BCI systems rely on electroencephalography (EEG) signals, which are susceptible to noise, artifacts, and low signal-to-noise ratios (SNR). This can lead to inaccurate decoding of neural activity, hindering the effectiveness of BCIs. Moreover, as EEG sensors are often placed non-invasively on the scalp, they may pick up electromagnetic interference (EMI) from nearby devices, further compromising signal quality.

To overcome these limitations, our team at Clisonix has developed an AI-powered approach to enhance EEG signal processing, leveraging cutting-edge technologies such as the CRDT Merge Layer and Post-Quantum Encryption.
