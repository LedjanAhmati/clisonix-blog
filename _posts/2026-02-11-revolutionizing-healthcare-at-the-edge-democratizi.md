**The Problem: Real Challenges in Edge Computing**

Edge computing has gained significant traction in recent years due to its ability to process data closer to where it's generated. In healthcare, this proximity is crucial for real-time decision-making, especially in emergency situations. However, traditional edge computing architectures often rely on centralized models, which can lead to:

1. **Latency**: Delays between data generation and processing can be detrimental in medical emergencies.
2. **Scalability**: Centralized systems can become bottlenecks as the number of devices increases.
3. **Security**: The aggregation of sensitive patient data at a central location poses significant security risks.
