As healthcare applications increasingly rely on electroencephalography (EEG) signals to monitor and diagnose neurological conditions, the need for robust data protection has never been more pressing. EEG data is particularly sensitive due to its direct correlation with an individual's brain activity, making it a prime target for unauthorized access or misuse.

**The Problem**

EEG data protection challenges are multifaceted:

1.  **Signal complexity**: EEG signals are highly complex and require specialized processing techniques, making them vulnerable to errors and tampering.
2.  **Security threats**: Healthcare applications are frequent targets of cyberattacks, compromising sensitive patient data and potentially leading to serious consequences.
3.  **Regulatory compliance**: Healthcare organizations must adhere to strict data protection regulations, such as HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act), which imposes severe penalties for non-compliance.
