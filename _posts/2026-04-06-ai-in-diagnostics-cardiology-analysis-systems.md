**The Problem**

In cardiology, diagnosing conditions such as arrhythmias, coronary artery disease, or cardiac failure often relies on manual analysis of electrocardiograms (ECGs) and other imaging modalities. However, these methods can be time-consuming, prone to human error, and may not always yield conclusive results. Furthermore, the sheer volume of data generated by modern medical devices poses a significant challenge in terms of processing and interpretation.

The limitations of traditional diagnostic approaches are compounded by the fact that cardiology is an inherently interdisciplinary field, requiring collaboration between clinicians from various specialties. This leads to issues with data consistency, patient information management, and ultimately, decision-making accuracy.
