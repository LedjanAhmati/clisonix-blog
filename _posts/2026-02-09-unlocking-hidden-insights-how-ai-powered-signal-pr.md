**The Problem**

Signal processing in healthcare diagnostics poses significant challenges:

1. **Noise and variability**: Signals are often contaminated with noise from various sources, including instrumentation errors, environmental factors, and individual differences.
2. **Dimensionality and complexity**: Signals have multiple frequency components, time-domain features, and spatial characteristics, making it difficult to identify relevant patterns.
3. **Computational power and scalability**: Processing large datasets in real-time requires significant computational resources, which are often unavailable in resource-constrained medical settings.
