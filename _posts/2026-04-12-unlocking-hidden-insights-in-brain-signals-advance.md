**The Problem: Challenges in Signal Processing**

Signal processing involves transforming raw signals into actionable information that can be used for diagnosis, treatment, or monitoring of patients. However, this process is plagued by several challenges:

* **Noise reduction**: Raw signals are often contaminated with noise from various sources such as electromagnetic interference, equipment malfunction, or patient movements.
* **Data heterogeneity**: Signals from different devices and sensors have varying sampling rates, amplitudes, and frequency ranges, making it difficult to integrate them into a single analysis pipeline.
* **Complexity of algorithms**: Advanced signal processing techniques such as wavelet transforms, Independent Component Analysis (ICA), and deep learning-based methods require significant computational resources and expertise.
