**The Problem**

While traditional methods rely on self-reported questionnaires or clinician-assessment interviews, these approaches have limitations. Many patients struggle to articulate their feelings, leading to misdiagnoses or delayed treatment. Moreover, mental health conditions are often characterized by subtle changes in behavior and physiology that can be difficult to detect.

Audio biomarkers offer a unique opportunity to bridge this gap. By analyzing the acoustic characteristics of speech, we can identify patterns indicative of depression. Research has shown that individuals with depression exhibit distinct audio features, such as increased vocal tremor, slower speech rate, and altered prosody (the rhythm and melody of speech).
