**The Problem: Real Challenges in Edge Computing**

Traditionally, AI applications have been cloud-centric, relying on centralized processing power to analyze vast amounts of data. However, this approach poses several challenges:

1.  **Latency**: Data must be transmitted from the edge to the cloud and back, resulting in delayed decision-making.
2.  **Bandwidth**: High-resolution medical imaging and sensor data require significant bandwidth, making it difficult to transmit over long distances.
3.  **Security**: As data is transmitted between devices, there's a higher risk of breaches and unauthorized access.

To overcome these hurdles, healthcare institutions must leverage edge computing platforms that empower local AI processing, reducing reliance on cloud infrastructure.
