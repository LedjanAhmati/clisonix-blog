**The Problem: Real Challenges in Audio Processing**

Audio processing is a complex task that involves extracting meaningful information from sound waves. In medical diagnostics, audio signals are used to analyze various physiological parameters such as heart rate, breathing patterns, and other vital signs. However, these signals often contain noise, artifacts, and inconsistencies that can affect the accuracy of diagnoses.

One significant challenge in audio processing is the presence of background noise. This can be due to various factors such as environmental conditions, equipment malfunction, or even patient movement. Another challenge is signal degradation, which occurs when the audio signal is transmitted over long distances or stored on digital media for extended periods.

To overcome these challenges, researchers and clinicians have been exploring the use of deep learning algorithms in audio processing. These algorithms can learn to identify patterns in audio signals and improve their quality by filtering out noise and artifacts.
