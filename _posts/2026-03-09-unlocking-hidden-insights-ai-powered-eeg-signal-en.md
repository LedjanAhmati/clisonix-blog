In the field of medical diagnostics, electroencephalography (EEG) has become an essential tool for monitoring brain activity and detecting neurological disorders. However, the accuracy of EEG signals is heavily dependent on their quality, which can be severely compromised by various artifacts such as noise, interference, and hardware limitations. This problem is further exacerbated by the vast amount of data generated in real-time, making it challenging to extract meaningful insights from these signals.

**The Problem**

Signal processing is a critical step in EEG analysis, but traditional methods often fall short in addressing the complexities of biomedical data. Most existing approaches rely on manual filtering techniques or simple algorithms that fail to account for the intricate relationships between different frequency bands and signal components. As a result, the resulting processed signals can be inaccurate, leading to misdiagnoses and incorrect treatments.
