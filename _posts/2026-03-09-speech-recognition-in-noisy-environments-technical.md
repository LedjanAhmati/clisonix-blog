**The Problem**

Audio processing in noisy environments poses a significant technical hurdle for speech recognition systems. Background noise can come from various sources such as ventilation systems, traffic, or even ambient chatter, making it difficult for algorithms to accurately identify spoken words. This issue is particularly prevalent in healthcare settings where patients may be receiving treatment while speaking with clinicians.

To develop effective speech recognition in noisy environments, we must delve into the technical nuances of audio processing. At its core, this involves understanding how algorithms handle signal-to-noise ratio (SNR), noise robustness, and feature extraction.
